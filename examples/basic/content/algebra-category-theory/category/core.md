A **category** \(\mathcal C\) consists of objects, morphisms between objects,
identity morphisms, and a composition law for morphisms.

The axioms say that composition is associative and that identity morphisms act
as left and right units.

Categories package mathematical structures by focusing on their maps and the
rules for composing those maps.
