A **Hilbert space** is an {{< knowl id="inner-product-space" text="inner product space" >}} $(H,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle)$ that is complete with respect to the induced norm $\|x\|=\sqrt{\langle x,x\rangle}$.

With this induced norm, every Hilbert space is a {{< knowl id="banach-space" text="Banach space" >}}. The {{< knowl id="cauchy-schwarz-inequality" text="Cauchyâ€“Schwarz inequality" >}} is a fundamental tool for relating inner products to norms in Hilbert space geometry.

**Examples:**
- $\mathbb{R}^n$ with the usual dot product.
- For a {{< knowl id="measure-space" section="measure-theory" text="measure space" >}} $(X,\Sigma,\mu)$, the space $L^2(X,\mu)$ of square-integrable functions with inner product $\langle f,g\rangle=\int_X f\,\overline{g}\,d\mu$.
