---
title: "Galois group"
description: "The group of field automorphisms of L that fix K pointwise, Gal(L/K)."
---

**Definition.** Let \(L/K\) be a {{< knowl id="field-extension" text="field extension" >}}. The **Galois group** of \(L/K\) is
\[
\mathrm{Gal}(L/K) \;=\; \{\sigma\in \mathrm{Aut}(L)\;:\;\sigma(a)=a\ \text{for all } a\in K\},
\]
a subgroup of the {{< knowl id="field-automorphism" text="field automorphism group" >}} of \(L\). With composition, \(\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) is a {{< knowl id="group" section="algebra-groups" text="group" >}}.

When \(L/K\) is {{< knowl id="galois-extension" text="Galois" >}}, \(\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) encodes intermediate fields via the {{< knowl id="galois-correspondence" text="Galois correspondence" >}}.

**See also.** {{< knowl id="fixed-field" text="fixed field" >}}, {{< knowl id="automorphism-group" section="algebra-groups" text="automorphism group" >}}.

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{C}/\mathbb{R})=\{ \mathrm{id}, \text{complex conjugation}\}\cong C_2\).
2. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt2)/\mathbb{Q})\cong C_2\), sending \(\sqrt2\mapsto \pm\sqrt2\).
3. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p)\) is cyclic of order \(n\), generated by \(x\mapsto x^p\) (see {{< knowl id="finite-field-galois-group-cyclic" text="finite-field Galois groups are cyclic" >}}).
