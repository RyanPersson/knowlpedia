---
title: "Field extension"
description: "An inclusion K ⊆ L of fields (equivalently, an injective field homomorphism K → L)."
---

**Definition.** A **field extension** \(L/K\) consists of fields \(K\) and \(L\) together with an injective field homomorphism \(\iota:K\hookrightarrow L\). Identifying \(K\) with \(\iota(K)\), one usually writes \(K\subseteq L\) and says “\(L\) is an extension field of \(K\)”.

When \(K\subseteq L\), the field \(L\) is naturally a {{< knowl id="vector-space" section="shared-linear-algebra" text="vector space" >}} over \(K\). If \(\dim_K L\) is finite, it is the {{< knowl id="degree-of-extension" text="degree of the extension" >}} \([L:K]\).

**See also.** {{< knowl id="field-embedding" text="field embedding" >}}, {{< knowl id="intermediate-field" text="intermediate field" >}}, {{< knowl id="tower-of-fields" text="tower of fields" >}}.

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathbb{R}\subseteq \mathbb{C}\) is a field extension; in fact \([\mathbb{C}:\mathbb{R}]=2\).
2. \(\mathbb{Q}\subseteq \mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})\) is a field extension obtained by adjoining \(\sqrt2\).
3. \(\mathbb{F}_p\subseteq \mathbb{F}_{p^n}\) is a finite field extension (see {{< knowl id="finite-field" text="finite fields" >}}).
