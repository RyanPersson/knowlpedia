**Definition.** Let \(K\) be a field and let \(\zeta_n\) be a {{< knowl id="primitive-root-of-unity" text="primitive n-th root of unity" >}} in some extension field. The extension
\[
K(\zeta_n)/K
\]
is called a **cyclotomic extension**. It is a {{< knowl id="simple-extension" text="simple extension" >}} generated by a root of the {{< knowl id="cyclotomic-polynomial" text="cyclotomic polynomial" >}} \(\Phi_n(x)\).

Over \(K=\mathbb{Q}\), the cyclotomic field \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)\) is {{< knowl id="galois-extension" text="Galois" >}} over \(\mathbb{Q}\), and its Galois group is abelian (in fact isomorphic to \((\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times\)).

**See also.** {{< knowl id="galois-group" text="Galois group" >}}, {{< knowl id="splitting-field" text="splitting field" >}}.

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_3)=\mathbb{Q}\!\left(\frac{-1+i\sqrt3}{2}\right)=\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-3})\), a quadratic extension.
2. \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_4)=\mathbb{Q}(i)\), also quadratic.
3. \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)/\mathbb{Q}\) has degree \(4\) since \(\deg \Phi_5=\varphi(5)=4\).
