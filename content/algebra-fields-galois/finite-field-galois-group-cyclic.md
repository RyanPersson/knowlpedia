---
title: "Galois Group of a Finite Field Extension is Cyclic"
description: "Gal(F_{p^n}/F_p) is cyclic, generated by Frobenius."
---

**Theorem.**  
Let \(q=p^n\) and let \(\mathbb{F}_q\) be the finite field with \(q\) elements. The extension \(\mathbb{F}_q/\mathbb{F}_p\) is {{< knowl id="galois-extension" text="Galois" >}}, and
\[
\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_q/\mathbb{F}_p)\cong C_n
\]
is cyclic of order \(n\), generated by the {{< knowl id="frobenius-endomorphism" text="Frobenius map" >}} \(\varphi(x)=x^p\).

More generally, \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{q^m}/\mathbb{F}_q)\) is cyclic of order \(m\), generated by \(x\mapsto x^q\) (see {{< knowl id="finite-field-galois-cyclic" text="finite-field Galois cyclicity" >}}).

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^2}/\mathbb{F}_p)=\{1,\varphi\}\) where \(\varphi(x)=x^p\).
2. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{2^3}/\mathbb{F}_2)\) is cyclic of order \(3\); its elements are \(1,\varphi,\varphi^2\).
3. For \(\mathbb{F}_{q^6}/\mathbb{F}_q\), the Galois group is cyclic of order \(6\), generated by \(x\mapsto x^q\).
