---
title: "Galois extension"
description: "An algebraic extension that is both normal and separable."
---

**Definition.** An {{</* knowl id="algebraic-extension" text="algebraic extension" */>}} \(L/K\) is **Galois** if it is both
- {{</* knowl id="normal-extension" text="normal" */>}}, and
- {{</* knowl id="separable-extension" text="separable" */>}}.

In this case the {{</* knowl id="galois-group" text="Galois group" */>}} \(\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) controls the field via the {{</* knowl id="fundamental-theorem-galois-theory" text="fundamental theorem of Galois theory" */>}}. For finite Galois extensions, one has \(|\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)|=[L:K]\) (see {{</* knowl id="galois-degree-equals-group-order" text="degree equals group order" */>}}).

**See also.** {{</* knowl id="fixed-field" text="fixed field" */>}}, {{</* knowl id="galois-correspondence" text="Galois correspondence" */>}}.

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathbb{C}/\mathbb{R}\) is Galois; \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{C}/\mathbb{R})\cong C_2\).
2. \(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p\) is Galois (indeed cyclic), generated by the {{</* knowl id="frobenius-endomorphism" text="Frobenius" */>}} automorphism.
3. The splitting field of \(x^3-2\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\), namely \(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt[3]{2},\zeta_3)\), is Galois over \(\mathbb{Q}\).
