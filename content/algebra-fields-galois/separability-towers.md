---
title: "Separability in Towers"
description: "Separability behaves well under passing up and down a tower of fields."
---

**Theorem.**  
Let \(K \subseteq L \subseteq M\) be a tower of fields with \(M/K\) algebraic.

1. If \(M/K\) is {{< knowl id="separable-extension" text="separable" >}}, then \(M/L\) and \(L/K\) are separable.
2. If \(L/K\) and \(M/L\) are separable, then \(M/K\) is separable.

In the finite case, these statements combine naturally with the {{< knowl id="tower-law" text="tower law" >}} for degrees.

**Examples.**
1. If \(K\) has characteristic \(0\), then every algebraic extension is separable; hence all steps in any algebraic tower over \(K\) are separable.
2. Finite fields: \(\mathbb{F}_p \subseteq \mathbb{F}_{p^d} \subseteq \mathbb{F}_{p^n}\) are all separable extensions (see {{< knowl id="finite-fields-perfect" text="finite fields are perfect" >}}).
3. If \(L/K\) is separable and \(\alpha\) is separable over \(L\), then \(\alpha\) is separable over \(K\), so \(L(\alpha)/K\) is separable.
