---
title: "Riemann integrability implies boundedness"
description: "A Riemann integrable function on a closed interval must be bounded."
---

**Riemann integrability implies boundedness:** Let $a<b$ and let $f:[a,b]\to\mathbb{R}$ be a {{< knowl id="riemann-integrable-function" text="Riemann integrable function" >}}. Then $f$ is bounded on $[a,b]$ (equivalently, $f$ is both {{< knowl id="bounded-above" text="bounded above" >}} and {{< knowl id="bounded-below" text="bounded below" >}}).

This is a necessary condition for the {{< knowl id="riemann-integral" text="Riemann integral" >}} to be well-defined, since {{< knowl id="upper-sum" text="upper sums" >}} and {{< knowl id="lower-sum" text="lower sums" >}} take suprema and infima of $f$ on subintervals of a {{< knowl id="partition-of-an-interval" text="partition" >}}.
