---
title: "Partition of an interval"
description: "A finite increasing sequence of points that subdivides a closed interval."
---

A **partition of an interval** $[a,b]$ is a finite sequence of real numbers
\[
P=\{x_0,x_1,\dots,x_n\}\quad\text{with}\quad a=x_0<x_1<\cdots<x_n=b.
\]
The associated subintervals are $[x_{i-1},x_i]$ for $i=1,\dots,n$.

Partitions of the {{< knowl id="interval" text="interval" >}} $[a,b]$ are used to build {{< knowl id="riemann-sum" text="Riemann sums" >}} and the {{< knowl id="upper-sum" text="upper" >}}/{{< knowl id="lower-sum" text="lower sums" >}} that define the {{< knowl id="riemann-integral" text="Riemann integral" >}}.

**Examples:**
- The uniform partition of $[0,1]$ into $n$ pieces is $P=\{0,\tfrac1n,\tfrac2n,\dots,1\}$.
- A non-uniform partition of $[0,3]$ is $P=\{0,\tfrac12,1,3\}$.
