---
title: "Interval"
description: "A subset of the real line that contains every point between any two of its points."
---

An **interval** is a {{< knowl id="subset" section="shared-foundations" text="subset" >}} $I\subseteq\mathbb R$ such that whenever $a,c\in I$ and $a<b<c$, then $b\in I$.

Intervals are the basic “connected pieces” of the ordered real line (using the {{< knowl id="order-axioms" text="order axioms" >}}). In the usual topology on $\mathbb R$ coming from the {{< knowl id="metric" section="topology" text="metric" >}} $d(x,y)=|x-y|$, open intervals are fundamental examples of {{< knowl id="open-set" section="topology" text="open sets" >}}.

**Examples:**
- $(0,1)=\{x\in\mathbb R: 0<x<1\}$ is an interval.
- $[0,\infty)=\{x\in\mathbb R: x\ge 0\}$ is an interval.
