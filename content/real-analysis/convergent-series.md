---
title: "Convergent series"
description: "A series whose partial sums approach a finite limit."
---

A **convergent series** is a {{< knowl id="series" text="series" >}} $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n$ whose {{< knowl id="partial-sums" text="partial sums" >}} $s_n=\sum_{k=1}^n a_k$ converge to a finite limit $S$, in which case one writes $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n = S$.

Equivalently, the partial sums form a {{< knowl id="convergent-sequence" section="topology" text="convergent sequence" >}} in $\mathbb{R}$. Two important refinements are {{< knowl id="absolutely-convergent-series" text="absolute convergence" >}} and {{< knowl id="conditionally-convergent-series" text="conditional convergence" >}}.

**Examples:**
- $\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{2^n}$ converges (in fact, to $2$).
- For $|r|<1$, the geometric series $\sum_{n=0}^\infty r^n$ converges (to $\frac{1}{1-r}$).
