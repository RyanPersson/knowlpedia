---
title: "Riemann–Stieltjes integrability theorem"
description: "Continuity of the integrand and bounded variation of the integrator guarantee Riemann–Stieltjes integrability."
---

**Riemann–Stieltjes integrability theorem:** Let $a<b$. If $f:[a,b]\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and $g:[a,b]\to\mathbb{R}$ is a {{< knowl id="bounded-variation-function" text="bounded variation function" >}}, then $f$ is {{< knowl id="riemann-stieltjes-integral" text="Riemann–Stieltjes integrable" >}} with respect to the {{< knowl id="integrator-function" text="integrator function" >}} $g$ on $[a,b]$.

Since every {{< knowl id="monotone-function" text="monotone function" >}} has bounded variation, this theorem extends existence of the {{< knowl id="riemann-integral" text="Riemann integral" >}} (the case $g(x)=x$) to a wider class of integrators.
