---
title: "Monotone Sequence Convergence Theorem"
description: "Every bounded monotone real sequence converges, with limit given by a supremum or infimum."
---

**Monotone sequence convergence theorem:** Let $(a_n)$ be a {{< knowl id="monotone-sequence" text="monotone sequence" >}} of real numbers.

- If $(a_n)$ is increasing and {{< knowl id="bounded-above" text="bounded above" >}}, then $(a_n)$ converges and
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n=\sup\{a_n:\ n\in\mathbb{N}\}.
\]
- If $(a_n)$ is decreasing and {{< knowl id="bounded-below" text="bounded below" >}}, then $(a_n)$ converges and
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n=\inf\{a_n:\ n\in\mathbb{N}\}.
\]

This theorem is a primary working form of the {{< knowl id="completeness-axiom" text="completeness axiom" >}} and is used throughout real analysis to produce limits from order and boundedness information.
