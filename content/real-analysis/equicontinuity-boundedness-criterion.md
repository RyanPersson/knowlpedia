---
title: "Equicontinuity–boundedness criterion"
description: "On a compact metric space, equicontinuity plus pointwise boundedness implies uniform boundedness."
---

**Equicontinuity–boundedness criterion:** Let $K$ be a compact {{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology" text="metric space" >}} and let $\mathcal{F}$ be a family of functions $f:K\to\mathbb{R}$. Assume $\mathcal{F}$ is an {{< knowl id="equicontinuous-family" text="equicontinuous family" >}} and is {{< knowl id="pointwise-bounded-family" text="pointwise bounded" >}} on $K$. Then $\mathcal{F}$ is {{< knowl id="uniformly-bounded-family" text="uniformly bounded" >}} on $K$.

This lemma is a standard compactness-based tool used alongside {{< knowl id="arzela-ascoli-theorem" text="Arzelà–Ascoli" >}} when working in the {{< knowl id="supremum-norm" text="supremum norm" >}}.
