---
title: "Conditionally convergent series"
description: "A convergent series that is not absolutely convergent."
---

A **conditionally convergent series** is a series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n$ that is {{< knowl id="convergent-series" text="convergent" >}} but not {{< knowl id="absolutely-convergent-series" text="absolutely convergent" >}}, meaning that $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n$ converges while $\sum_{n=1}^\infty |a_n|$ diverges.

Conditional convergence is fragile under {{< knowl id="rearrangement-of-series" text="rearrangements" >}}: the {{< knowl id="riemann-rearrangement-theorem" text="Riemann rearrangement theorem" >}} shows that rearranging terms can change the sum or destroy convergence. Many standard examples are produced using the {{< knowl id="alternating-series-test" text="alternating series test" >}}.

**Examples:**
- The alternating harmonic series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{n}$ converges conditionally.
- The alternating series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n-1}}{\sqrt{n}}$ converges conditionally.
