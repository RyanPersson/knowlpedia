---
title: "Riemann linearity"
description: "Linearity of the Riemann integral with respect to addition and scalar multiplication."
---

**Riemann linearity:** Let $f,g:[a,b]\to\mathbb R$ be {{< knowl id="riemann-integrable-function" text="Riemann integrable" >}} on the {{< knowl id="interval" text="interval" >}} $[a,b]$, and let $\alpha,\beta\in\mathbb R$. Then $\alpha f+\beta g$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$, and
$$
\int_a^b (\alpha f+\beta g)=\alpha\int_a^b f+\beta\int_a^b g.
$$

This is the basic linear structure behind the {{< knowl id="riemann-integral" text="Riemann integral" >}} and is used throughout the algebraic manipulation of integrals.
