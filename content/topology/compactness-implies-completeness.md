---
title: "Compactness implies completeness"
description: "A compact metric space is complete: every Cauchy sequence converges."
---

**Compactness implies completeness:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{< knowl id="metric-space" text="metric space" >}} and let $K\subseteq X$ be {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}} (with the induced metric). Then $K$ is a {{< knowl id="complete-metric-space" text="complete metric space" >}}: every {{< knowl id="cauchy-sequence" text="Cauchy sequence" >}} in $K$ is a {{< knowl id="convergent-sequence" text="convergent sequence" >}} with limit in $K$.

Together with {{< knowl id="compactness-implies-total-boundedness" text="compactness implies total boundedness" >}}, this yields the metric equivalence {{< knowl id="compact-iff-complete-totally-bounded" text="compact iff complete and totally bounded" >}}.
