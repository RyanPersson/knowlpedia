---
title: "Linear Closure Equals Topological Closure for Solid Convex Sets"
description: "For convex sets with nonempty interior in a normed space, lin(立)=cl(立)."
---

Let $X$ be a {{< knowl id="norm-normed-vector-space" text="normed vector space" >}} and let $\Omega\subset X$ be {{< knowl id="convex-set" text="convex" >}}, with {{< knowl id="interior-of-a-set" text="int(立)" >}}$\neq\emptyset$.

**Proposition**:
$$
\operatorname{lin}(\Omega)=\overline{\Omega},
$$
where $\overline{\Omega}$ denotes the {{< knowl id="closure-of-a-set" text="closure" >}} of $\Omega$ in the norm-induced topology.

**Context:**
This result says that, for "solid" convex sets, the algebraic construction {{< knowl id="linear-closure" text="lin(立)" >}} (built from segments) recovers the usual topological closure. Compare with {{< knowl id="core-equals-interior-for-convex-sets-in-normed-spaces" text="core equals interior" >}} for the analogous result on the interior side.
