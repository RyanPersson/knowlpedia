---
title: "Measurable space"
description: "A set equipped with a sigma-algebra of measurable subsets."
---

A **measurable space** is a pair $(X,\Sigma)$ consisting of a {{< knowl id="set" section="shared-foundations" text="set" >}} $X$ and a {{< knowl id="sigma-algebra" text="sigma-algebra" >}} $\Sigma$ on $X$.

Measurable spaces are the domains and codomains for {{< knowl id="measurable-function" text="measurable functions" >}}; adding a {{< knowl id="measure" text="measure" >}} produces a {{< knowl id="measure-space" text="measure space" >}}.

**Examples:**
- $(\mathbb R,\mathcal B(\mathbb R))$, where $\mathcal B(\mathbb R)$ is the {{< knowl id="borel-sigma-algebra" text="Borel sigma-algebra" >}} on $\mathbb R$.
- $(X,\mathcal P(X))$, where $\mathcal P(X)$ is the {{< knowl id="power-set" section="shared-foundations" text="power set" >}} of $X$.
