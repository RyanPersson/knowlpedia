---
title: "Simple module"
description: "A nonzero module with no proper nontrivial submodules."
---

A (left) $R$-{{</* knowl id="module" text="module" */>}} $M\ne 0$ is **simple** if its only {{</* knowl id="submodule" text="submodules" */>}} are $0$ and $M$. Equivalently, $M$ has no {{</* knowl id="proper-subset" text="proper" */>}} nonzero submodule.

Simple modules are the analogs of “atoms” in module theory: they appear as factors in composition series, and semisimplicity is built from direct sums of simples. Over a commutative ring, simple modules are precisely $R/\mathfrak m$ where $\mathfrak m$ is a {{</* knowl id="maximal-ideal" text="maximal ideal" */>}}, hence are vector spaces over the field $R/\mathfrak m$ (compare {{</* knowl id="quotient-module" text="quotients" */>}}

**Examples:**
- As a $\mathbb Z$-module, $\mathbb Z/p\mathbb Z$ is simple for prime $p$.
- If $k$ is a field, then any 1-dimensional $k$-vector space is a simple $k$-module.
- (Nonexample) $\mathbb Z/4\mathbb Z$ is not simple as a $\mathbb Z$-module because it has the nontrivial submodule $2\mathbb Z/4\mathbb Z$.
