---
title: "Kernels are submodules"
description: "The kernel of a module homomorphism is a submodule of its domain."
---

**Kernels are submodules**: If $f:M\to N$ is an $R$-module homomorphism, then $\ker(f)\le M$.

In particular, the {{</* knowl id="kernel-module" text="kernel" */>}} is always a canonical {{</* knowl id="submodule" text="submodule" */>}} attached to a {{</* knowl id="module-homomorphism" text="module homomorphism" */>}}, and it is the input for forming the {{</* knowl id="quotient-module" text="quotient by the kernel" */>}}.
