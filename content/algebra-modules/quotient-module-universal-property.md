---
title: "Universal property of quotient modules"
description: "A map that kills a submodule factors uniquely through the quotient."
---

**Universal property of quotient modules**: Let $M$ be an $R$-module, let $N\le M$ be a submodule, and let $\pi:M\to M/N$ be the quotient map. For any $R$-module $Q$ and any homomorphism $f:M\to Q$ such that $N\subseteq \ker(f)$, there exists a unique homomorphism $\bar f:M/N\to Q$ with $f=\bar f\circ \pi$.

This is the defining mapping property of the {{< knowl id="quotient-module" text="quotient module" >}}, and it expresses quotients as the universal way to force a {{< knowl id="submodule" text="submodule" >}} to lie in the {{< knowl id="kernel-module" text="kernel" >}} of a {{< knowl id="module-homomorphism" text="module homomorphism" >}}.
