---
title: "Rank of a free module"
description: "The cardinality of a basis of a free module."
---

Let $F$ be a {{< knowl id="free-module" text="free module" >}}. The **rank** of $F$ is the {{< knowl id="cardinality" section="shared-foundations" text="cardinality" >}} of a {{< knowl id="basis-module" text="basis" >}}, denoted $\operatorname{rank}(F)$. This is well-defined: any two bases of a free module have the same cardinality.

When the rank is finite, it plays the role of {{< knowl id="dimension" text="dimension" >}}, but over a general ring one typically speaks of rank only for free (or locally free) modules.

**Examples:**
- $\operatorname{rank}(R^n)=n$.
- The zero module has rank $0$ (its basis is the empty set).
- If $F\cong \bigoplus_{i\in I} R$, then $\operatorname{rank}(F)=|I|$.
