A **symplectic Hilbert space** is a real {{< knowl id="hilbert-space" section="linear-algebra" text="Hilbert space" >}} \(K\) with a continuous
{{< knowl id="symplectic-form" text="symplectic form" >}} \(B\).

In Shale's paper, \(K\) comes from a complex \(H\) by realification, with \(B=\Im(\cdot,\cdot)_c\).

**Key properties:**
- Linear symmetries are elements of {{< knowl id="symplectic-group-spk" text="Sp(K)" >}}.
- Implementability in Fock space depends on "restricted" size conditions (Hilbertâ€“Schmidt).

**Example:** \(K=H_{\mathbb R}\) for complex \(H\), \(B(z_1,z_2)=\Im(z_1,z_2)_c\).
