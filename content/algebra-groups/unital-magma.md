---
title: "Unital Magma"
description: "A magma with an identity element"
---

A **unital magma** is a {{< knowl id="magma" text="magma" >}} $(M, \cdot)$ equipped with an **identity element** $e \in M$ satisfying
$$e \cdot a = a \cdot e = a$$

for all $a \in M$.

This is the weakest structure requiring an identity. All {{< knowl id="monoid" text="monoids" >}}, {{< knowl id="loop" text="loops" >}}, and {{< knowl id="group" text="groups" >}} are unital magmas.

**Examples:**
- Any monoid, loop, or group
- A set with a non-associative operation that still has an identity element
