**Fields and trivial ideals**: Let $R$ be a commutative ring with $1$. Then $R$ is a field if and only if the only ideals of $R$ are $(0)$ and $R$. Equivalently, every nonzero element of $R$ is a unit.

This criterion characterizes [[algebra-rings/field|fields]] among [[algebra-rings/commutative-ring|commutative rings]] via the lattice of [[algebra-rings/ideal|ideals]]; the forward direction uses that nonzero elements are [[algebra-rings/unit|units]], and the reverse direction shows every nonzero principal ideal must be the whole ring. It is often paired with [[algebra-rings/maximal-iff-quotient-field|maximal iff quotient is a field]] to analyze [[algebra-rings/maximal-ideal|maximal ideals]].
