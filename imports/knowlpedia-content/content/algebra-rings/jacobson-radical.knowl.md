Let $R$ be a ring. The **Jacobson radical** $J(R)$ is the intersection of all [[algebra-rings/maximal-ideal|maximal ideals]] of $R$ (in the commutative case), and more generally can be defined as the intersection of annihilators of all simple right $R$-modules.

In a unital ring, an element $a$ lies in $J(R)$ iff for every $r\in R$ the element $1-ar$ is a [[algebra-rings/unit|unit]]. The Jacobson radical is always an [[algebra-rings/ideal|ideal]], and $R/J(R)$ captures the “semisimple part” of $R$.

**Examples:**
- For $R=\mathbb Z$, one has $J(R)=\{0\}$.
- For a local ring $(R,\mathfrak m)$, $J(R)=\mathfrak m$.
- For $R=\mathbb Z/p^k\mathbb Z$, $J(R)$ is the ideal generated by the class of $p$.
