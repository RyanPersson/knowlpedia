**PID ⇒ UFD**: If \(R\) is a [[algebra-rings/pid|principal ideal domain]], then \(R\) is a [[algebra-rings/ufd|unique factorization domain]].

A key step is that in a PID every [[algebra-rings/irreducible-element|irreducible element]] is [[algebra-rings/prime-element|prime]], because \((p)\) is maximal among proper principal ideals containing \(p\). Existence of factorizations follows from the ascending chain condition on principal ideals.
