**Characteristic of an integral domain is 0 or prime**: If $D$ is an integral domain, then $\operatorname{char}(D)$ is either $0$ or a prime number $p$.

The proof analyzes the kernel of the canonical map $\mathbb Z\to D$ using [[algebra-rings/kernel-is-ideal|the kernel–ideal property]], and thereby determines the [[algebra-rings/characteristic|characteristic]] of an [[algebra-rings/integral-domain|integral domain]]. This dichotomy is the starting point for constructing the smallest subfield inside any [[algebra-rings/field|field]] (see the corollary on prime subfields).
