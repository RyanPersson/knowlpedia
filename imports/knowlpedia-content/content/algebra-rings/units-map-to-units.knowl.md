**Units map to units**: Let $\varphi:R\to S$ be a unital ring homomorphism. If $u\in R$ is a unit, then $\varphi(u)\in S$ is a unit and
\[
\varphi(u^{-1})=\varphi(u)^{-1}.
\]
Equivalently, $\varphi$ restricts to a group homomorphism $R^\times\to S^\times$ on unit groups.

For [[algebra-rings/unital-ring|unital rings]], any [[algebra-rings/ring-homomorphism|ring homomorphism]] induces a homomorphism of the [[algebra-rings/group-of-units|group of units]] by sending each [[algebra-rings/unit|unit]] to its image. In particular, any [[algebra-rings/ring-isomorphism|ring isomorphism]] identifies the unit groups of the two rings.
