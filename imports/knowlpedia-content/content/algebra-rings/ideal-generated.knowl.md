Given a [[shared-foundations/subset|subset]] $A\subseteq R$ of a [[algebra-rings/ring|ring]] $R$, the **ideal generated by $A$**, denoted $(A)$, is the smallest [[algebra-rings/ideal|ideal]] of $R$ containing $A$. Equivalently, $(A)$ consists of all finite sums of the form
\[
\sum_{i=1}^n r_i a_i s_i
\]
with $n\ge 1$, $a_i\in A$, and $r_i,s_i\in R$ (in the commutative case, one may take $s_i=1$).

When $A=\{a\}$ is a singleton, $(A)$ is a [[algebra-rings/principal-ideal|principal ideal]]. This construction is functorial in the sense that homomorphisms send generated ideals to generated ideals of images (up to containment).

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb Z$, the ideal generated by $\{6,10\}$ is $(2)$.
- In $k[x,y]$, the ideal generated by $\{x,y\}$ is $(x,y)$.
- In $M_2(k)$, the two-sided ideal generated by a nonzero matrix is all of $M_2(k)$.
