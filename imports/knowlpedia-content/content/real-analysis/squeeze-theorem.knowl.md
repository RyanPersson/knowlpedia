**Squeeze theorem:** Let $(a_n)$, $(b_n)$, and $(c_n)$ be real sequences such that $a_n \le b_n \le c_n$ for all sufficiently large $n$. If $a_n \to L$ and $c_n \to L$, then $b_n \to L$.

This theorem is a standard tool for proving convergence without explicitly computing a limit, and it has an analogous form for [[real-analysis/limit-at-a-point|limits at a point]] of functions.
