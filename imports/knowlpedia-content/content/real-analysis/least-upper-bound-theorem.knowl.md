**Least Upper Bound Theorem**: If $E\subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is nonempty and [[real-analysis/bounded-above|bounded above]], then $\sup E$ exists in $\mathbb{R}$.

This theorem is the working form of [[real-analysis/completeness-axiom|completeness]]: it guarantees the existence of optimal bounds and is used to prove convergence of [[real-analysis/monotone-sequence|monotone sequences]], the existence of limits, and many properties of continuous functions.
