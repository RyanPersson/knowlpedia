**Terms go to zero:** If the [[real-analysis/series|series]] $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n$ [[real-analysis/convergent-series|converges]], then
\[
\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n = 0.
\]

Equivalently (the divergence test), if $a_n$ does not tend to $0$, then the series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n$ is [[real-analysis/divergent-series|divergent]]. This is a basic prerequisite for many convergence tests and is often used to quickly rule out convergence before applying finer criteria like the [[real-analysis/root-test|root test]] or [[real-analysis/ratio-test|ratio test]].
