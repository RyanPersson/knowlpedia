**Uniform limit theorem for continuity**: Let $(X,d)$ be a [[topology/metric-space|metric space]] and let $(f_n)$ be a sequence of [[real-analysis/continuity-on-a-set|continuous]] functions $f_n:X\to\mathbb{R}$ (or into any metric space). If $f_n\to f$ [[real-analysis/uniform-convergence-of-a-sequence-of-functions|uniformly]] on $X$, then $f$ is continuous on $X$.

[[real-analysis/uniform-convergence-of-a-sequence-of-functions|Uniform convergence]] is strong enough to pass continuity through the limit. This is a fundamental reason uniform convergence is preferred over [[real-analysis/pointwise-convergence|pointwise convergence]] in analysis.
