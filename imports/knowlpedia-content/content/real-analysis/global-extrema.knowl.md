**Global extrema:** Let $K\subseteq\mathbb R^n$ be nonempty and compact, and let $f:K\to\mathbb R$ be [[topology/continuous-map|continuous]]. Then there exist points $x_{\min},x_{\max}\in K$ such that
$$
f(x_{\min})\le f(x)\le f(x_{\max}) \quad \text{for all } x\in K.
$$

In particular, $f$ has a [[real-analysis/minimum|minimum]] and a [[real-analysis/maximum|maximum]] on $K$.

On a closed [[real-analysis/interval|interval]] $[a,b]$, this theorem ensures the existence of global maximizers and minimizers needed in many basic arguments in differential and integral calculus.
