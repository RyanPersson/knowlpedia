**Archimedean property:** For every real number $x$ there exists $n \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $n > x$. Equivalently, for every $\varepsilon>0$ there exists $n \in \mathbb{N}$ such that $0<\frac{1}{n}<\varepsilon$.

This property is a basic consequence of the interaction between the [[real-analysis/order-axioms|order axioms]] and the [[real-analysis/completeness-axiom|completeness axiom]]. It is used repeatedly in approximation arguments, for example in the [[real-analysis/density-of-q|density of the rationals]].
