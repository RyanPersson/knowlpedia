**Continuous implies Riemann integrable:** Let $a<b$. If $f:[a,b]\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous (as a [[topology/continuous-map|continuous map]]), then $f$ is a [[real-analysis/riemann-integrable-function|Riemann integrable function]] on $[a,b]$.

In practice, this guarantees the existence of the [[real-analysis/riemann-integral|Riemann integral]] for most elementary functions, and it interacts well with the [[real-analysis/mean-value-theorem-for-integrals|mean value theorem for integrals]] and the [[real-analysis/fundamental-theorem-of-calculus-i|fundamental theorem of calculus]].
