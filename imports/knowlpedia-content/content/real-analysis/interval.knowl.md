An **interval** is a [[shared-foundations/subset|subset]] $I\subseteq\mathbb R$ such that whenever $a,c\in I$ and $a<b<c$, then $b\in I$.

Intervals are the basic “connected pieces” of the ordered real line (using the [[real-analysis/order-axioms|order axioms]]). In the usual topology on $\mathbb R$ coming from the [[topology/metric|metric]] $d(x,y)=|x-y|$, open intervals are fundamental examples of [[topology/open-set|open sets]].

**Examples:**
- $(0,1)=\{x\in\mathbb R: 0<x<1\}$ is an interval.
- $[0,\infty)=\{x\in\mathbb R: x\ge 0\}$ is an interval.
