A **(reduced) root system** is a finite subset $\Phi\subset V\setminus\{0\}$ of a real finite-dimensional inner product space $(V,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle)$ such that:

1. $\Phi$ spans $V$.
2. If $\alpha\in\Phi$, then the only scalar multiples of $\alpha$ in $\Phi$ are $\pm\alpha$.
3. For each $\alpha\in\Phi$, the reflection
   \[
   s_\alpha(v)=v-\frac{2\langle v,\alpha\rangle}{\langle \alpha,\alpha\rangle}\,\alpha
   \]
   preserves $\Phi$, i.e. $s_\alpha(\Phi)=\Phi$.
4. (**Integrality**) For all $\alpha,\beta\in\Phi$,
   \[
   \frac{2\langle \beta,\alpha\rangle}{\langle \alpha,\alpha\rangle}\in\mathbb Z.
   \]

The subgroup of $\mathrm{GL}(V)$ generated by the reflections $\{s_\alpha\}$ is the [[lie-groups/weyl-group|Weyl group]] of $\Phi$.

Root systems arise from semisimple Lie algebras: if $\mathfrak g$ is semisimple and $\mathfrak h$ is a [[lie-groups/cartan-subalgebra|Cartan subalgebra]], then the set of roots $\Phi\subset \mathfrak h^*$ (see [[lie-groups/root-lie-algebra|root of a Lie algebra]]) becomes a root system after identifying $\mathfrak h^*$ with a real inner product space via the [[lie-groups/killing-form|Killing form]]. The corresponding [[lie-groups/root-space-decomposition|root space decomposition]] is the bridge between algebra and combinatorics.

Choosing a [[lie-groups/positive-root|positive system]] picks out a basis of [[lie-groups/simple-root|simple roots]], from which one constructs the [[lie-groups/cartan-matrix|Cartan matrix]] and [[lie-groups/dynkin-diagram|Dynkin diagram]]. This data underlies the [[lie-groups/classification-simple-lie-algebras|classification of simple Lie algebras]].
