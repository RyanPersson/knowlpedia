A finite field extension $K/k$ is **separable** if every element of $K$ has a separable minimal polynomial over $k$.

It is **normal** if every $k$-embedding $K\hookrightarrow \bar k$ has image $K$.

It is **Galois** if it is both normal and separable; then the **Galois group** is
$
\mathrm{Gal}(K/k):=\mathrm{Aut}_k(K).
$

**In the letter:** $\Gamma=\mathrm{Gal}(K/k)$ acts on split data and is used in [[langlands-letter/knowls/galois-descent-forms|descent/twisting]] and in the [[langlands-letter/knowls/l-group-satake-parameter|$L$-group]].
