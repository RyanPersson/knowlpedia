**Continuous attains max/min on compact set:** Let $K$ be a [[topology/compact-set|compact set]] and let $f:K\to\mathbb{R}$ be a [[topology/continuous-map|continuous map]]. Then there exist points $x_{\min},x_{\max}\in K$ such that
$f(x_{\min})\le f(x)\le f(x_{\max})$ for all $x\in K$.

Equivalently, the subset $f(K)\subseteq\mathbb{R}$ has both a [[real-analysis/minimum|minimum]] and a [[real-analysis/maximum|maximum]]. This can be seen by combining [[topology/continuous-image-of-compact-set-is-compact|compactness of continuous images]] with basic order properties of compact subsets of $\mathbb{R}$.
