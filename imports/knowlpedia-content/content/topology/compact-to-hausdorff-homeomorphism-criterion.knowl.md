**Compact-to-Hausdorff homeomorphism criterion:** Let $f:X\to Y$ be a [[topology/continuous-map|continuous map]] that is bijective. If $X$ is [[topology/compact-set|compact]] and $Y$ is [[topology/hausdorff-space|Hausdorff]], then $f$ is a [[topology/homeomorphism|homeomorphism]] (equivalently, $f^{-1}:Y\to X$ is continuous).

A common way to use this is via the fact that in a Hausdorff space, [[topology/compact-subset-of-hausdorff-is-closed|compact sets are closed]]; in particular, under the hypotheses above, images of closed sets in $X$ are closed in $Y$, so $f$ is a closed map.
