**Totally bounded implies Cauchy subsequence:** Let $(X,d)$ be a [[topology/metric-space|metric space]] that is [[topology/totally-bounded-set|totally bounded]]. Then every sequence $(x_n)$ in $X$ has a [[real-analysis/subsequence|subsequence]] $(x_{n_k})$ that is a [[topology/cauchy-sequence|Cauchy sequence]].

This is a key step in the compactness criterion “complete + totally bounded,” and it is closely related to constructing finite [[topology/epsilon-net|epsilon-nets]] at decreasing scales.
