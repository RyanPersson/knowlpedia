**Continuous image of compact set is compact:** Let $f:X\to Y$ be a [[topology/continuous-map|continuous map]] between [[topology/topological-space|topological spaces]]. If $K\subseteq X$ is [[topology/compact-set|compact]], then $f(K)$ is compact in $Y$, where $f(K)$ is the [[shared-foundations/image|image]] of $K$ under the [[shared-foundations/function|function]] $f$.

This is one of the most important invariance properties in topology; it underlies results like [[topology/continuous-attains-max-min-compact|attainment of maxima and minima on compact domains]].
