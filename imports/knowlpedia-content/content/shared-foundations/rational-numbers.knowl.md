The **rational numbers** are the [[shared-foundations/set|set]] $\mathbb{Q}$ of fractions $p/q$ with $p,q\in\mathbb{Z}$ and $q\ne 0$, where $p/q$ and $p'/q'$ represent the same rational number exactly when $pq'=p'q$.

One way to formalize this identification is to view $\mathbb{Q}$ as a [[shared-foundations/quotient-set|quotient set]] of [[shared-foundations/ordered-pair|ordered pairs]] of [[shared-foundations/integers|integers]] under an [[shared-foundations/equivalence-relation|equivalence relation]]. The rationals embed into the [[shared-foundations/real-numbers|real numbers]].

**Examples:**
- $\frac{2}{3}$ and $-\frac{7}{4}$ are rational numbers.
- Every integer $n$ corresponds to the rational number $n/1$.
