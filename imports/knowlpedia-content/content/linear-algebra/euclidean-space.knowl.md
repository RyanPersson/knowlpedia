A **Euclidean space** is a real inner product space $(V,\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle)$ with $\dim V<\infty$.

Equivalently, it is a finite-dimensional [[linear-algebra/inner-product-space|inner product space]] (hence a finite-dimensional [[linear-algebra/vector-space|vector space]]) over $\mathbb{R}$. The inner product determines lengths via the [[linear-algebra/euclidean-norm|Euclidean norm]] and the notion of perpendicularity via [[linear-algebra/orthogonality|orthogonality]].

**Examples:**
- $\mathbb{R}^n$ with the dot product $\langle x,y\rangle=\sum_{i=1}^n x_i y_i$.
- Any linear subspace $W\subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$ with the dot product restricted to $W$.
