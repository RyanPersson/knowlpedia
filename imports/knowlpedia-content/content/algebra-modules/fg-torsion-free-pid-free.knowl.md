**Finitely generated torsion-free over a PID is free**: Let $R$ be a PID and let $M$ be a finitely generated torsion-free $R$-module. Then $M$ is a free $R$-module.

This is the torsion-free specialization of the [[algebra-modules/structure-theorem-pid|structure theorem for finitely generated modules over a PID]]: for a [[algebra-rings/pid|PID]], a [[algebra-modules/finitely-generated-module|finitely generated]] [[algebra-modules/torsion-free-module|torsion-free module]] has no torsion summand and must therefore be [[algebra-modules/free-module|free]].
