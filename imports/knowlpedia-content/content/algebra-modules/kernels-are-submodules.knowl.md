**Kernels are submodules**: If $f:M\to N$ is an $R$-module homomorphism, then $\ker(f)\le M$.

In particular, the [[algebra-modules/kernel-module|kernel]] is always a canonical [[algebra-modules/submodule|submodule]] attached to a [[algebra-modules/module-homomorphism|module homomorphism]], and it is the input for forming the [[algebra-modules/quotient-module|quotient by the kernel]].
