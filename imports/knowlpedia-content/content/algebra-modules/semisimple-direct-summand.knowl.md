**Semisimple iff every submodule is a direct summand**: For an $R$-module $M$, the following are equivalent:
1. $M$ is semisimple.
2. Every submodule $N\le M$ is a direct summand of $M$, i.e. there exists a submodule $N'\le M$ with $M = N\oplus N'$.
3. $M$ is (isomorphic to) a direct sum of simple submodules.

This gives the splitting characterization of [[algebra-modules/semisimple-module|semisimple modules]] in terms of [[algebra-modules/submodule|submodules]], and explains why such modules decompose as [[algebra-modules/direct-sum-modules|direct sums]] of [[algebra-modules/simple-module|simple modules]].
