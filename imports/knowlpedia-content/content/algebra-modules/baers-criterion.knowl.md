**Baer's criterion**: Let $R$ be a unital ring and let $Q$ be a left $R$-module. Then $Q$ is injective if and only if for every left ideal $I\subseteq R$ and every $R$-linear map $f:I\to Q$, there exists an $R$-linear map $F:R\to Q$ such that $F|_I=f$.

This reduces checking [[algebra-modules/injective-module|injectivity]] to an extension property for [[algebra-modules/module-homomorphism|module homomorphisms]] defined on (left) [[algebra-rings/ideal|ideals]] of a [[algebra-rings/unital-ring|unital ring]].
