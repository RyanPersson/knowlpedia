Localization is built from a [[algebra-commutative/multiplicative-set|multiplicative set]] and formally inverts its elements (see [[algebra-commutative/localization-ring|localization]]). A fundamental finiteness fact is that Noetherianity survives this process; compare [[algebra-commutative/localization-noetherian|localization preserves Noetherianity]].

## Corollary (Noetherianity localizes)
Let \(R\) be a [[algebra-commutative/noetherian-ring|Noetherian ring]] and let \(S \subset R\) be a [[algebra-commutative/multiplicative-set|multiplicative set]]. Then the localization \(S^{-1}R\) is Noetherian.

In particular, for any prime ideal \(\mathfrak p\), the [[algebra-commutative/localization-at-prime|localization at \(\mathfrak p\)]] is Noetherian:
\(R_{\mathfrak p}\) is a Noetherian [[algebra-commutative/local-ring|local ring]]. Likewise, for any maximal ideal \(\mathfrak m\), \(R_{\mathfrak m}\) is a Noetherian local ring in the sense of [[algebra-commutative/maximal-ideal-local-ring|local ring with maximal ideal]].

## Examples
1. **Localizing the integers.**
   \(\mathbb Z\) is Noetherian, so for any prime \(p\) the localization \(\mathbb Z_{(p)}\) is Noetherian. Concretely, \(\mathbb Z_{(p)}\) consists of fractions \(a/b\) with \(p\nmid b\), and its unique maximal ideal is generated by \(p\).

2. **Inverting a polynomial.**
   If \(R=k[x,y]\) with \(k\) a field, then \(R\) is Noetherian by [[algebra-commutative/hilbert-basis-corollary|Hilbert basis]]. Localizing at the multiplicative set \(\{1,x,x^2,\dots\}\) gives \(k[x,y]_x\), which is again Noetherian.

3. **Localization can simplify quotients.**
   In \(A = k[x,y]/(xy)\), localize at powers of \(x\). Since \(x\) becomes invertible in \(A_x\), the relation \(xy=0\) forces \(y=0\) in the localization, so \(A_x \cong k[x,x^{-1}]\), a Noetherian ring.
