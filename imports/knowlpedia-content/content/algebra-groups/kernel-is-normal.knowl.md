**Proposition (Kernel is normal).**
Let $f:G\to H$ be a [[algebra-groups/group-homomorphism|group homomorphism]]. Let $\ker(f)$ be its [[algebra-groups/kernel-group|kernel]]. Then $\ker(f)$ is a [[algebra-groups/normal-subgroup|normal subgroup]] of $G$.

**Context.**
Normal subgroups arise naturally as kernels; conversely, every normal subgroup is the kernel of a canonical map to a quotient group.
