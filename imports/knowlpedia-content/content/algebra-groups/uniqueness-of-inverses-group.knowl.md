**Proposition (Uniqueness of inverses).**
Let $G$ be a [[algebra-groups/group|group]]. For $g\in G$, an element $x\in G$ is an **inverse** of $g$ if $xg=e$ and $gx=e$, where $e$ is the identity element of $G$.
If $x$ and $y$ are both inverses of $g$, then $x=y$.

**Context.**
This justifies writing $g^{-1}$ for *the* inverse of $g$.
