A **loop** is a [[algebra-groups/quasigroup|quasigroup]] with an identity element. That is, a set $L$ with a binary operation such that:

1. **Identity:** There exists $e \in L$ with $ea = ae = a$ for all $a \in L$
2. **Divisibility:** For all $a, b \in L$, the equations $ax = b$ and $ya = b$ have unique solutions

A loop that is also associative is a [[algebra-groups/group|group]]. The smallest non-associative loop has 5 elements.

**Examples:**
- Unit octonions under multiplication (non-associative!)
- Moufang loops
- All groups are loops
