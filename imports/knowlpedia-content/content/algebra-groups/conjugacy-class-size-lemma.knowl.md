**Conjugacy Class Size Lemma**: Let $G$ be a finite [[algebra-groups/group|group]] and let $g\in G$. Let $\mathrm{Cl}(g)$ denote the [[algebra-groups/conjugacy-class|conjugacy class]] of $g$, and let $C_G(g)$ denote the [[algebra-groups/centralizer|centralizer]] of $g$. Then
$
|\mathrm{Cl}(g)| = [G : C_G(g)],
$
where $[G:C_G(g)]$ is the [[algebra-groups/index-of-subgroup|index]] of $C_G(g)$ in $G$.

This is an instance of the [[algebra-groups/orbit-stabilizer-theorem|orbit–stabilizer theorem]] applied to the [[algebra-groups/conjugation-action|conjugation action]] of $G$ on itself.
