Let $G$ be a [[algebra-groups/group|group]] and let $N,H\le G$ be [[algebra-groups/subgroup|subgroups]]. One says that **$G$ is the internal semidirect product of $N$ and $H$** if:
1. $N$ is a [[algebra-groups/normal-subgroup|normal subgroup]],
2. $N\cap H=\{e\}$,
3. $NH=G$.

Conjugation by elements of $H$ defines a homomorphism $H\to \operatorname{Aut}(N)$ (coming from the [[algebra-groups/conjugation-action|conjugation action]]), and with respect to this map one has an isomorphism
$$
G \cong N \rtimes H,
$$
so internal semidirect products are precisely the internal realizations of [[algebra-groups/semidirect-product|semidirect products]].

**Examples:**
- $S_3$ is an internal semidirect product of $A_3$ (normal, order $3$) and $\langle(12)\rangle$ (order $2$), hence $S_3\cong C_3\rtimes C_2$.
- $D_{2n}$ is an internal semidirect product of its rotation subgroup $C_n$ and a reflection subgroup $C_2$.
- If, in addition, $H$ is normal, then $G$ is an [[algebra-groups/internal-direct-product|internal direct product]].
