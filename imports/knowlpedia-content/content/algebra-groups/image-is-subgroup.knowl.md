**Proposition (Image is a subgroup).**
Let $f:G\to H$ be a [[algebra-groups/group-homomorphism|group homomorphism]]. Let $\mathrm{im}(f)$ be its [[algebra-groups/image-group|image]]. Then $\mathrm{im}(f)$ is a [[algebra-groups/subgroup|subgroup]] of $H$.

**Context.**
Together with "kernel is normal," this gives the basic structural picture of any homomorphism: it factors through a quotient of $G$ onto a subgroup of $H$.
