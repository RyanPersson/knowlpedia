**A p-group has nontrivial center**: Let $G$ be a finite [[algebra-groups/p-group|p-group]], i.e. $|G|=p^n$ for some prime $p$ and integer $n\ge 1$. Then the [[algebra-groups/center-of-group|center]] $Z(G)$ is nontrivial; in fact, $|Z(G)|$ is divisible by $p$, so $|Z(G)|\ge p$.

This is a standard application of the [[algebra-groups/class-equation|class equation]], which decomposes $|G|$ into the size of the center plus sizes of non-central conjugacy classes.
