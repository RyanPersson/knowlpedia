Let $X$ be a [[convex-analysis/norm-normed-vector-space|normed space]] over $\mathbb{R}$.

The **(continuous) dual space** $X^\ast $ is the set of all continuous linear functionals $x^\ast :X\to\mathbb{R}$ (equivalently, all [[convex-analysis/bounded-linear-functional-norm-of-a-functional|bounded linear functionals]] on $X$).

For $x^\ast \in X^\ast $ and $x\in X$, the **duality pairing** is
$$
\langle x^\ast ,x\rangle:=x^\ast (x).
$$

This pairing is the standard language used in separation theorems such as [[convex-analysis/separation-by-a-closed-hyperplane|separation by a closed hyperplane]] and [[convex-analysis/strict-separation-by-a-closed-hyperplane|strict separation]].
