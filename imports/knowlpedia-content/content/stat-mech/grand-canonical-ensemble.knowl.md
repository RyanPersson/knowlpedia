The **grand canonical ensemble** models a system that can exchange both energy and particles with a reservoir, so temperature and chemical potential are fixed while energy and particle number fluctuate.

It is the natural ensemble for open systems, lattice gases, and quantum gases, and it provides a direct route to the thermodynamic [[thermodynamics/grand-potential|grand potential]] and pressure.

Fix an inverse temperature [[thermodynamics/inverse-temperature-beta|inverse temperature]] $\beta>0$ and a [[thermodynamics/chemical-potential-thermo|chemical potential]] $\mu$. For each particle number $N$, let $\Gamma_N$ be the $N$-particle [[stat-mech/phase-space-classical|phase space]] (or Hilbert space sector), and let $H_N$ be the corresponding [[stat-mech/hamiltonian-function-classical|Hamiltonian]].

The grand canonical weight of a microstate $x\in\Gamma_N$ is proportional to $e^{-\beta(H_N(x)-\mu N)}$. The normalized probability density is
$$
\rho_{\beta,\mu}(N,x)
\;=\;
\frac{e^{-\beta(H_N(x)-\mu N)}}{\Xi(\beta,\mu)},
$$
where the normalizing constant
$$
\Xi(\beta,\mu)
\;=\;
\sum_{N\ge 0}\int_{\Gamma_N} e^{-\beta(H_N(x)-\mu N)}\, d\Gamma_N
$$
is the [[stat-mech/grand-partition-function|grand partition function]] (also called the grand canonical partition function).

(Quantum version: $\Xi(\beta,\mu)=\mathrm{Tr}\,\exp\{-\beta(\hat H-\mu \hat N)\}$.)

## Ensemble averages
For an observable $A$ that may depend on $N$ and $x$, the [[stat-mech/ensemble-average|ensemble average]] is
$$
\langle A\rangle_{\beta,\mu}
\;=\;
\sum_{N\ge 0}\int_{\Gamma_N} A(N,x)\,\rho_{\beta,\mu}(N,x)\, d\Gamma_N.
$$

## Derivative formulas (mean values and fluctuations)
Thermodynamic observables are generated by derivatives of $\ln\Xi$ (see [[stat-mech/construction-observables-from-log-z|observables from log partition functions]]):

- Mean particle number:
$$
\langle N\rangle_{\beta,\mu}
\;=\;
\frac{1}{\beta}\,\frac{\partial}{\partial\mu}\ln \Xi(\beta,\mu).
$$

- Particle-number fluctuations:
$$
\mathrm{Var}_{\beta,\mu}(N)
\;=\;
\frac{1}{\beta^2}\,\frac{\partial^2}{\partial\mu^2}\ln \Xi(\beta,\mu),
$$
which connects directly to response coefficients such as compressibility (a special case of [[stat-mech/susceptibility-stat-mech|susceptibility–fluctuation relations]]).

- Mean energy:
$$
\left\langle E\right\rangle_{\beta,\mu}
\;=\;
-\frac{\partial}{\partial\beta}\ln \Xi(\beta,\mu) \;+\; \mu\,\langle N\rangle_{\beta,\mu}.
$$

More generally, mixed derivatives of $\ln\Xi$ generate covariances, fitting into [[stat-mech/construction-fluctuation-formulas-log-z|fluctuation formulas from log partition functions]].

## Grand potential and pressure
The thermodynamic grand potential is obtained from $\Xi$ by
$$
\Omega(\beta,\mu)
\;=\;
-\frac{1}{\beta}\ln \Xi(\beta,\mu),
$$
matching the [[thermodynamics/grand-potential|thermodynamic grand potential]] and arising as a [[stat-mech/construction-legendre-f-to-omega|Legendre transform of free energy]] in equilibrium thermodynamics.

For a homogeneous system of volume $V$, one has $\Omega = -pV$, so the pressure can be read from the partition function as in [[stat-mech/pressure-from-partition-function|pressure from the partition function]]:
$$
p
\;=\;
\frac{1}{\beta V}\,\ln \Xi(\beta,\mu).
$$

## Relation to other ensembles
If particle-number fluctuations are suppressed (e.g., by conditioning on a fixed $N$), the grand canonical ensemble reduces to the [[stat-mech/canonical-ensemble|canonical ensemble]]. In the [[thermodynamics/thermodynamic-limit|thermodynamic limit]], predictions for many bulk observables often coincide across these ensembles under suitable conditions (ensemble equivalence).
