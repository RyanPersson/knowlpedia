Let \(K\) be a finite [[algebra-rings/field|field]] of characteristic \(p>0\). The Frobenius map
\[
\mathrm{Fr}:K\to K,\quad x\mapsto x^p
\]
is a ring endomorphism (see [[algebra-fields-galois/frobenius-endomorphism|Frobenius endomorphism]]) and is automatically injective; finiteness forces it to be surjective, hence an automorphism. Therefore every element of \(K\) has a unique \(p\)-th root in \(K\), and more generally every algebraic extension of \(K\) is [[algebra-fields-galois/separable-extension|separable]]. In other words:

**Theorem.** Every finite field is [[algebra-fields-galois/perfect-field|perfect]].

Equivalently, every irreducible polynomial over a finite field has distinct roots in an [[algebra-fields-galois/algebraic-closure|algebraic closure]] (cf. [[algebra-fields-galois/separable-distinct-roots|separable polynomials have distinct roots]]).

### Examples

1. **Prime fields.**
   \(\mathbb{F}_p\) is perfect: \(\mathrm{Fr}(x)=x^p\) is the identity map on \(\mathbb{F}_p\), hence an automorphism.

2. **Degree-\(n\) finite fields.**
   For \(K=\mathbb{F}_{p^n}\), Frobenius is an automorphism of order \(n\), and the extension \(K/\mathbb{F}_p\) is [[algebra-fields-galois/galois-extension|Galois]] with cyclic [[algebra-fields-galois/galois-group|Galois group]] generated by Frobenius (see [[algebra-fields-galois/finite-field-galois-group-cyclic|finite-field Galois group is cyclic]]).

3. **Contrast with an infinite non-perfect field.**
   \(\mathbb{F}_p(t)\) is not perfect: \(t\) has no \(p\)-th root in \(\mathbb{F}_p(t)\), and \(x^p-t\) defines an inseparable finite extension (as in [[algebra-fields-galois/finite-extension-perfect-separable|perfect implies separable]]).
