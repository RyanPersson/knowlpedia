Let \(F\) be a [[algebra-rings/field|field]].

**Definition (algebraic closure).** An *algebraic closure* of \(F\) is a field \(\overline F\) equipped with an inclusion \(F\subseteq \overline F\) such that:
1. \(\overline F/F\) is an [[algebra-fields-galois/algebraic-extension|algebraic extension]], i.e. every element of \(\overline F\) is algebraic over \(F\);
2. \(\overline F\) is *algebraically closed*: every nonconstant polynomial in \(\overline F[x]\) splits completely into linear factors over \(\overline F\).

In particular, every \(f(x)\in F[x]\) splits in \(\overline F[x]\), so \(\overline F\) contains a [[algebra-fields-galois/splitting-field|splitting field]] for every polynomial over \(F\).

Existence and uniqueness (up to \(F\)-isomorphism) are treated in [[algebra-fields-galois/algebraic-closure-existence|existence of algebraic closures]] and [[algebra-fields-galois/algebraic-closure-uniqueness|uniqueness of algebraic closures]]. A useful consequence is that any algebraic [[algebra-fields-galois/field-extension|field extension]] \(K/F\) admits an \(F\)-[[algebra-fields-galois/field-embedding|embedding]] into \(\overline F\).

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathbb{C}\) is an algebraic closure of \(\mathbb{R}\): it is algebraically closed, and \(\mathbb{C}/\mathbb{R}\) is algebraic of degree \(2\) (generated by \(i\)).
2. Inside \(\mathbb{C}\), the set \(\overline{\mathbb{Q}}\) of algebraic numbers (complex numbers algebraic over \(\mathbb{Q}\)) forms an algebraic closure of \(\mathbb{Q}\).
3. For a [[algebra-fields-galois/finite-field|finite field]] \(\mathbb{F}_p\), one can realize an algebraic closure as
   \[
   \overline{\mathbb{F}_p}=\bigcup_{n\ge 1}\mathbb{F}_{p^n}
   \]
   inside a fixed large field, where \(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}\) ranges over all finite extensions of \(\mathbb{F}_p\).
