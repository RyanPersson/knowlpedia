Let \(K/F\) be a [[algebra-fields-galois/field-extension|field extension]].

**Definition (Galois group).** The *Galois group* of \(K/F\), denoted \(\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)\), is the set of all [[algebra-fields-galois/field-automorphism|field automorphisms]] \(\sigma:K\to K\) that fix \(F\) pointwise (i.e., \(\sigma(a)=a\) for all \(a\in F\)).
With composition as the operation, \(\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)\) is a group (a subgroup of the [[algebra-groups/automorphism-group|automorphism group]] of the field \(K\)).

When \(K/F\) is a finite [[algebra-fields-galois/galois-extension|Galois extension]], one has \(|\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)|=[K:F]\), and the base field \(F\) is the [[algebra-fields-galois/fixed-field|fixed field]] of \(\mathrm{Gal}(K/F)\).

**Examples.**
1. \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt2)/\mathbb{Q})\) has two elements: the identity and the automorphism sending \(\sqrt2\mapsto -\sqrt2\). Thus it is cyclic of order \(2\).
2. For finite fields, \(\mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{F}_{p^n}/\mathbb{F}_p)\) is cyclic of order \(n\), generated by the Frobenius automorphism \(x\mapsto x^p\) (compare [[algebra-fields-galois/finite-field-galois-group-cyclic|finite-field Galois groups are cyclic]]).
3. For a [[algebra-fields-galois/cyclotomic-extension|cyclotomic extension]] \(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q}\), every automorphism is determined by \(\zeta_n\mapsto \zeta_n^a\) with \(\gcd(a,n)=1\), giving an isomorphism
   \[
   \mathrm{Gal}(\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)/\mathbb{Q}) \cong (\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^\times.
   \]
