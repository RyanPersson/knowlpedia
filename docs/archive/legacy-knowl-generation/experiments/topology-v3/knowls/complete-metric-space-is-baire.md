---
title: "Complete metric space is Baire"
description: "A complete metric space, with its metric topology, is a Baire space."
---

**Complete metric space is Baire:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="complete-metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="complete metric space" >}}}, and let $X$ carry the {{{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" section="topology-metric" text="topology induced by" >}}} $d$. Then $X$ is a {{{< knowl id="baire-space" text="Baire space" >}}}.

This is a standard formulation of the {{{< knowl id="baire-category-theorem" text="Baire category theorem" >}}} and is frequently used to show that certain “good” sets are {{{< knowl id="residual-set" text="residual" >}}} (equivalently, that the set of exceptions is {{{< knowl id="meager-set" text="meager" >}}}).
