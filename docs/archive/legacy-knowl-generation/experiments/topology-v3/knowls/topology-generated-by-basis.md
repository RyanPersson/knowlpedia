---
title: "Topology generated by a basis"
description: "The unique topology whose open sets are unions of basis elements."
---

Let $X$ be a set and let $\mathcal{B}$ be a {{{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis" >}}} on $X$. The **topology generated by** $\mathcal{B}$ is the collection
\[
\tau_{\mathcal{B}}=\{U\subseteq X : \text{for every } x\in U \text{ there exists } B\in\mathcal{B} \text{ with } x\in B\subseteq U\}.
\]
Equivalently, $\tau_{\mathcal{B}}$ consists of all {{{< knowl id="union" section="shared-foundations" text="unions" >}}} of elements of $\mathcal{B}$.

With this topology, the elements of $\mathcal{B}$ are {{{< knowl id="open-set" text="open" >}}}, and every open set is obtained by gluing basis elements together.
