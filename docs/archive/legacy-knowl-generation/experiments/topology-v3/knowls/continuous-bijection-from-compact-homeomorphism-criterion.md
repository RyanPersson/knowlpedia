---
title: "Continuous bijection from compact is a homeomorphism criterion"
description: "A continuous bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space is a homeomorphism"
---

**Compact-to-Hausdorff homeomorphism criterion:** Let $X$ and $Y$ be {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological spaces" >}}}, and let $f\colon X\to Y$ be a {{{< knowl id="continuous-map" section="topology-core" text="continuous map" >}}} that is {{{< knowl id="bijective-function" section="shared-foundations" text="bijective" >}}}. If $X$ is {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}} and $Y$ is {{{< knowl id="hausdorff-space" section="topology-separation" text="Hausdorff" >}}}, then $f$ is a {{{< knowl id="homeomorphism" section="topology-core" text="homeomorphism" >}}}.

Equivalently, under these hypotheses the inverse map $f^{-1}\colon Y\to X$ is continuous. A common route to this conclusion is that continuous images of compact sets are compact and compact subsets of Hausdorff spaces are {{{< knowl id="closed-set" section="topology-core" text="closed" >}}} (see {{{< knowl id="compact-subset-of-hausdorff-is-closed" text="compact subset of Hausdorff is closed" >}}}).
