---
title: "Compact set"
description: "A set for which every open cover has a finite subcover"
---

A **compact set** $K$ in a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}}} $X$ is a subset such that every {{{< knowl id="open-cover" text="open cover" >}}} of $K$ has a finite subcover.

Compactness is a topological finiteness condition that interacts strongly with continuity (see {{{< knowl id="continuous-image-of-compact-set-is-compact" text="continuous images of compact sets" >}}}) and separation properties (see {{{< knowl id="compact-subset-of-hausdorff-is-closed" text="compact sets in Hausdorff spaces" >}}}).

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb R$ with its usual topology, the interval $[0,1]$ is compact (by the {{{< knowl id="heine-borel-theorem" text="Heine--Borel theorem" >}}}).
- In $\mathbb R$, the open interval $(0,1)$ is not compact.
