---
title: "Compactness implies total boundedness"
description: "In a metric space, every compact set is totally bounded"
---

**Compactness implies total boundedness:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}. Then $K$ is {{{< knowl id="totally-bounded-set" text="totally bounded" >}}}.

Together with {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-completeness" text="compactness implies completeness" >}}}, this gives one direction of the metric characterization {{{< knowl id="compact-iff-complete-totally-bounded" text="compact iff complete and totally bounded" >}}}.
