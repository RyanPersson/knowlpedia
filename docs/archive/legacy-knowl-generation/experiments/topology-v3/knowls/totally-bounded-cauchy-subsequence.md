---
title: "Totally bounded sets yield Cauchy subsequences"
description: "Every sequence in a totally bounded set has a Cauchy subsequence"
---

**Totally bounded implies Cauchy subsequence:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}} and let $A\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="totally-bounded-set" text="totally bounded" >}}}. Then every sequence in $A$ has a subsequence that is a {{{< knowl id="cauchy-sequence" section="topology-metric" text="Cauchy sequence" >}}} in $X$.

This is one of the key steps in showing that “complete + totally bounded” implies {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}} (see {{{< knowl id="compact-iff-complete-totally-bounded" text="compact iff complete and totally bounded" >}}}).
