---
title: "Compactness implies completeness"
description: "A compact metric space is complete, and compact subsets are complete in the subspace metric"
---

**Compactness implies completeness:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}. Then $K$ is complete with respect to the metric induced from $d$; equivalently, every {{{< knowl id="cauchy-sequence" section="topology-metric" text="Cauchy sequence" >}}} in $K$ {{{< knowl id="convergent-sequence" section="topology-metric" text="converges" >}}} to a point of $K$.

This is one half of the characterization {{{< knowl id="compact-iff-complete-totally-bounded" text="compact iff complete and totally bounded" >}}} for metric spaces.
