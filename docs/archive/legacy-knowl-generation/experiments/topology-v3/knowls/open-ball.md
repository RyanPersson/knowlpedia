---
title: "Open ball"
description: "The set of points within a strict distance r of a center point."
---

An **open ball** in a metric space $(X,d)$ is a set of the form
\[
B(x,r)=\{\,y\in X : d(x,y)<r\,\},
\]
where $x\in X$ and $r>0$.

Open balls are {{{< knowl id="neighborhood" section="topology-core" text="neighborhoods" >}}} of their centers and are {{{< knowl id="open-set" section="topology-core" text="open sets" >}}} in the {{{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" text="metric-induced topology" >}}}; in fact, they generate that topology.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual metric, $B(a,r)$ is the open interval $(a-r,a+r)$.
