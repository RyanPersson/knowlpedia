---
title: "Finite subcover lemma"
description: "An open cover of a compact set has a finite subcover"
---

**Finite subcover lemma:** Let $X$ be a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}. If $\mathcal U$ is an {{{< knowl id="open-cover" text="open cover" >}}} of $K$, then there exist $U_1,\dots,U_n\in\mathcal U$ such that $K\subseteq U_1\cup\cdots\cup U_n$.

This is the “finite subcover property,” and it is the defining feature of compactness in general topology.
