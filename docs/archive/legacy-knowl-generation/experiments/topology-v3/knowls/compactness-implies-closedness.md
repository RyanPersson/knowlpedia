---
title: "Compactness implies closedness"
description: "In a metric space, every compact set is closed"
---

**Compactness implies closedness:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}. Then $K$ is {{{< knowl id="closed-set" section="topology-core" text="closed" >}}} in $X$.

More generally, compact subsets of a {{{< knowl id="hausdorff-space" section="topology-separation" text="Hausdorff space" >}}} are closed; see {{{< knowl id="compact-subset-of-hausdorff-is-closed" text="compact subset of Hausdorff is closed" >}}}.
