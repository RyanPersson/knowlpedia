---
title: "Finite intersection property theorem"
description: "Compactness is equivalent to nonempty intersection for closed families with the finite intersection property"
---

**Finite intersection property theorem:** Let $X$ be a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$. The following are equivalent:
1. $K$ is {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}.
2. For every collection $\mathcal F$ of {{{< knowl id="closed-set" section="topology-core" text="closed" >}}} subsets of $K$ with the property that every finite subcollection has nonempty intersection, one has $\bigcap_{F\in\mathcal F} F\ne\varnothing$.

This recasts compactness in terms of intersections of closed sets and is often paired with cover-based arguments via complements of open sets.
