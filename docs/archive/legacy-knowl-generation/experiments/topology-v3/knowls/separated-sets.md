---
title: "Separated sets"
description: "Two subsets with strictly positive distance between them."
---

Two nonempty subsets $A,B$ of a metric space $(X,d)$ are **separated** if
\[
\operatorname{dist}(A,B)=\inf\{\,d(a,b): a\in A,\ b\in B\,\}>0.
\]

Separatedness is stronger than being disjoint: disjoint sets can have distance $0$ if they come arbitrarily close. This notion gives a quantitative way to talk about sets being apart in a metric sense.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual metric, $[0,1]$ and $[2,3]$ are separated (their distance is $1$).
- The disjoint sets $(0,1)$ and $(1,2)$ are not separated, since their distance is $0$.
