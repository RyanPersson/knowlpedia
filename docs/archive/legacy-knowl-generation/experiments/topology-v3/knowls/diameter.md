---
title: "Diameter"
description: "The supremum of distances between pairs of points in a set."
---

The **diameter** of a subset $A$ of a metric space $(X,d)$ is
\[
\operatorname{diam}(A)=\sup\{\,d(x,y): x,y\in A\,\}\in[0,\infty],
\]
with the convention $\operatorname{diam}(\varnothing)=0$.

Diameter packages the “size” of a set in a way that interacts well with {{{< knowl id="bounded-set" text="boundedness" >}}} and plays a key role in the {{{< knowl id="cantor-intersection-theorem" text="Cantor intersection theorem" >}}}.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual metric, $\operatorname{diam}([0,1])=1$.
