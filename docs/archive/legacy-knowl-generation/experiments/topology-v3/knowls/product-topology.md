---
title: "Product topology"
description: "The topology on a product space generated by products of open sets."
---

Let $X$ and $Y$ be {{{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological spaces" >}}}. The **product topology** on the {{{< knowl id="cartesian-product" section="shared-foundations" text="cartesian product" >}}} $X\times Y$ is the {{{< knowl id="topology" text="topology" >}}} generated by the {{{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis" >}}}
\[
\{U\times V : U \text{ is open in } X \text{ and } V \text{ is open in } Y\}.
\]

This topology is characterized by the fact that the coordinate projections $X\times Y\to X$ and $X\times Y\to Y$ are {{{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuous" >}}}, and it is the standard topology used to form products in topology.
