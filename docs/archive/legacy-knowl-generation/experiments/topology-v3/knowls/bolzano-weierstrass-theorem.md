---
title: "Bolzano–Weierstrass theorem"
description: "Every bounded sequence in Euclidean space has a convergent subsequence."
---

**Bolzano–Weierstrass theorem:** In {{{< knowl id="euclidean-space" section="linear-algebra" text="Euclidean space" >}}} $\mathbb{R}^n$ with the usual metric, every {{{< knowl id="bounded-set" text="bounded" >}}} sequence has a {{{< knowl id="convergent-sequence" text="convergent" >}}} subsequence.

This theorem is a fundamental sequential compactness principle in Euclidean spaces and is closely connected to the {{{< knowl id="heine-borel-theorem" section="topology-compactness" text="Heine–Borel theorem" >}}} and the general theory of {{{< knowl id="compact-set" section="topology-compactness" text="compact sets" >}}} in metric spaces.
