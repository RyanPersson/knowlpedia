---
title: "Dense subset"
description: "A subset that is dense in a specified subspace."
---

Let $Y\subseteq X$ be a subset of a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}}} $X$, and let $A\subseteq Y$. The set $A$ is a **dense subset** of $Y$ if $A$ is dense in $Y$ with respect to the {{{< knowl id="subspace-topology" text="subspace topology" >}}} on $Y$, i.e.
\[
\overline{A}^{\,Y}=Y,
\]
where $\overline{A}^{\,Y}$ denotes closure computed in the subspace $Y$.

Equivalently, $A$ is dense in $Y$ if $Y\subseteq \overline{A}$ where $\overline{A}$ is the {{{< knowl id="closure" text="closure" >}}} of $A$ in $X$.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual topology, $(0,1)$ is a dense subset of $[0,1]$.
