---
title: "Compactness implies boundedness"
description: "In a metric space, every compact set is bounded"
---

**Compactness implies boundedness:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}. Then $K$ is a {{{< knowl id="bounded-set" section="topology-metric" text="bounded set" >}}}.

This is one half of the familiar “closed and bounded” picture in Euclidean spaces; the full characterization is the {{{< knowl id="heine-borel-theorem" text="Heine--Borel theorem" >}}}.
