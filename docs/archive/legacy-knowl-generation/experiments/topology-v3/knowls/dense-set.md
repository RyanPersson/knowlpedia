---
title: "Dense set"
description: "A subset whose closure is the whole space."
---

A subset $A\subseteq X$ of a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}}} $X$ is **dense** in $X$ if its {{{< knowl id="closure" text="closure" >}}} equals $X$, i.e. $\overline{A}=X$.

Equivalently, $A$ is dense if every nonempty {{{< knowl id="open-set" text="open set" >}}} $U\subseteq X$ has nonempty {{{< knowl id="intersection" section="shared-foundations" text="intersection" >}}} with $A$.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual topology, $\mathbb{Q}$ is dense in $\mathbb{R}$.
