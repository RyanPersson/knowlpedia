---
title: "Heine--Borel theorem"
description: "In Euclidean space, compactness is equivalent to being closed and bounded"
---

**Heine--Borel theorem:** In $\mathbb R^n$ with its usual topology (equivalently, the metric from the {{{< knowl id="euclidean-norm" section="linear-algebra" text="Euclidean norm" >}}} on {{{< knowl id="euclidean-space" section="linear-algebra" text="Euclidean space" >}}}), a subset $K\subseteq \mathbb R^n$ is {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}} if and only if it is {{{< knowl id="closed-set" section="topology-core" text="closed" >}}} and {{{< knowl id="bounded-set" section="topology-metric" text="bounded" >}}}.

This specializes the metric facts {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-closedness" text="compact implies closed" >}}} and {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-boundedness" text="compact implies bounded" >}}} and provides their converse in Euclidean spaces.
