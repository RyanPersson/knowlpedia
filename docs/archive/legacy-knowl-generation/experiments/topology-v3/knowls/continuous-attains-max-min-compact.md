---
title: "Continuous functions attain maxima and minima on compact sets"
description: "A continuous real-valued function on a compact set has a maximum and a minimum"
---

**Extreme value theorem (compact form):** Let $X$ be a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}}}, let $K\subseteq X$ be {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}, and let $f\colon K\to\mathbb R$ be {{{< knowl id="continuous-map" section="topology-core" text="continuous" >}}} (where $\mathbb R$ has its usual topology). Then there exist points $x_{\min},x_{\max}\in K$ such that for all $x\in K$,
\[
f(x_{\min}) \le f(x) \le f(x_{\max}).
\]

A standard way to view this is that $f(K)$ is compact in $\mathbb R$ by {{{< knowl id="continuous-image-of-compact-set-is-compact" text="continuous image of compact is compact" >}}}, hence closed and bounded in $\mathbb R$ and therefore has a minimum and a maximum.
