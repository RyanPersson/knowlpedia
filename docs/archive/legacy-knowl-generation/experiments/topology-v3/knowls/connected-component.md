---
title: "Connected component"
description: "A maximal connected subset of a topological space, equivalently the union of all connected sets containing a point."
---

A **connected component** of a {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}}} $X$ is a subset $C\subseteq X$ that is {{{< knowl id="connected-set" text="connected" >}}} and maximal (under inclusion) among connected subsets of $X$.

Equivalently, for $x\in X$ the connected component of $x$ is the union of all connected subsets of $X$ that contain $x$; this union is the unique connected component containing $x$. The connected components form a {{{< knowl id="partition" section="shared-foundations" text="partition" >}}} of $X$.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}\setminus\{0\}$ with its {{{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" section="topology-metric" text="usual topology" >}}}, the connected components are $(-\infty,0)$ and $(0,\infty)$.
