---
title: "Homeomorphism"
description: "A bijective continuous map with continuous inverse."
---

A **homeomorphism** between {{{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological spaces" >}}} $X$ and $Y$ is a {{{< knowl id="bijective-function" section="shared-foundations" text="bijective" >}}} {{{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuous map" >}}} $f:X\to Y$ such that its {{{< knowl id="inverse-function" section="shared-foundations" text="inverse function" >}}} $f^{-1}:Y\to X$ is also continuous.

If there exists a homeomorphism between $X$ and $Y$, the spaces are said to be homeomorphic; this is the notion of “sameness” in topology.

**Examples:**
- The map $f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}$ given by $f(t)=\tan(\pi(t-\tfrac12))$ is a homeomorphism.
