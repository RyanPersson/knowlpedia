---
title: "Continuous images of compact sets are closed and bounded in metric spaces"
description: "A continuous image of a compact set in a metric space is compact, hence closed and bounded"
---

**Continuous image compact implies closed and bounded (metric target):** Let $f\colon X\to Y$ be a {{{< knowl id="continuous-map" section="topology-core" text="continuous map" >}}} between {{{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological spaces" >}}}, and assume $Y$ is a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}}. If $K\subseteq X$ is {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}}, then $f(K)\subseteq Y$ is compact, hence {{{< knowl id="closed-set" section="topology-core" text="closed" >}}} in $Y$ and {{{< knowl id="bounded-set" section="topology-metric" text="bounded" >}}} in $Y$.

This packages {{{< knowl id="continuous-image-of-compact-set-is-compact" text="continuous image of compact is compact" >}}} together with the metric facts {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-closedness" text="compact implies closed" >}}} and {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-boundedness" text="compact implies bounded" >}}}.
