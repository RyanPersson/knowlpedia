---
title: "Compact iff complete and totally bounded"
description: "A subset of a metric space is compact exactly when it is complete and totally bounded"
---

**Compact iff complete and totally bounded:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}} and let $K\subseteq X$. Then $K$ is {{{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}} if and only if:
- $K$ is complete (with the induced metric), and
- $K$ is {{{< knowl id="totally-bounded-set" text="totally bounded" >}}}.

The forward implication combines {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-completeness" text="compactness implies completeness" >}}} and {{{< knowl id="compactness-implies-total-boundedness" text="compactness implies total boundedness" >}}}; the reverse implication is typically proved using {{{< knowl id="totally-bounded-cauchy-subsequence" text="Cauchy subsequences from total boundedness" >}}}.
