---
title: "Lipschitz continuity"
description: "A quantitative continuity condition with a global linear bound."
---

A map $f:(X,d_X)\to(Y,d_Y)$ is **Lipschitz continuous** if there exists a constant $L\ge 0$ such that
\[
d_Y(f(x),f(y))\le L\,d_X(x,y)\quad\text{for all }x,y\in X.
\]

Lipschitz continuity is a strong form of {{{< knowl id="uniformly-continuous-map" text="uniform continuity" >}}} and is the special case $\alpha=1$ of {{{< knowl id="holder-continuity" text="Hölder continuity" >}}}.
