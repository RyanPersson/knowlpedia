---
title: "Subbasis of a topology"
description: "A collection of sets whose finite intersections form a basis."
---

A **subbasis** for a topology on a set $X$ is a collection $\mathcal{S}$ of {{{< knowl id="subset" section="shared-foundations" text="subsets" >}}} of $X$ such that the collection of all finite {{{< knowl id="intersection" section="shared-foundations" text="intersections" >}}} of elements of $\mathcal{S}$ forms a {{{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis" >}}}.

The topology generated by $\mathcal{S}$ consists of all {{{< knowl id="union" section="shared-foundations" text="unions" >}}} of finite intersections of members of $\mathcal{S}$, and is the smallest {{{< knowl id="topology" text="topology" >}}} on $X$ containing every set in $\mathcal{S}$.

**Examples:**
- For the {{{< knowl id="product-topology" text="product topology" >}}} on the {{{< knowl id="cartesian-product" section="shared-foundations" text="cartesian product" >}}} $X\times Y$, the sets of the form $U\times Y$ and $X\times V$ (with $U$ open in $X$ and $V$ open in $Y$) form a subbasis.
