---
title: "Connected subsets of R are intervals"
description: "Any connected subset of the real line with the usual topology must be an interval."
---

**Connected subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ are intervals:** Let $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}$, where $\mathbb{R}$ has its {{{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" section="topology-metric" text="usual topology" >}}}. If $A$ is {{{< knowl id="connected-set" text="connected" >}}}, then $A$ is an interval; explicitly, if $a,b\in A$ with $a<b$ and $t\in\mathbb{R}$ satisfies $a<t<b$, then $t\in A$.

Combined with {{{< knowl id="continuous-image-of-connected-set-is-connected" text="continuous images of connected sets being connected" >}}}, this yields the standard intermediate value phenomenon for continuous real-valued functions.
