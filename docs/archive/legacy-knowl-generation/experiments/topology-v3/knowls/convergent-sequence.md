---
title: "Convergent sequence"
description: "A sequence whose terms eventually get arbitrarily close to a point."
---

A **convergent sequence** in a metric space $(X,d)$ is a sequence $(x_n)$ for which there exists $x\in X$ such that for every $\varepsilon>0$ there is $N$ with $d(x_n,x)<\varepsilon$ for all $n\ge N$.

The point $x$ is the {{{< knowl id="limit-of-a-sequence" text="limit of the sequence" >}}}. In the {{{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" text="metric-induced topology" >}}}, this is equivalent to saying that for every {{{< knowl id="open-ball" text="open ball" >}}} around $x$, all sufficiently large terms lie in that ball.
