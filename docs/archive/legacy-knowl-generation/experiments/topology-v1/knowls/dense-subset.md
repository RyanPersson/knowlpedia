---
title: "Dense subset"
description: "A subset that is dense in the ambient space."
---

Let $(X,\tau)$ be a {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}}. A **dense subset** of $X$ is a {{< knowl id="subset" text="subset" >}} $A\subseteq X$ that is {{< knowl id="dense-set" text="dense" >}} in $X$ (equivalently, $\overline{A}=X$).

Dense subsets are used to reduce problems on $X$ to a “smaller” set while still controlling the whole space (for example, by checking properties on a dense set and extending by continuity when possible).

**Examples:**
- The rational numbers form a dense subset of $\mathbb{R}$ in the usual topology.
- Any space $X$ is a dense subset of itself.
- A proper dense subset can exist (e.g., $\mathbb{Q}\subsetneq \mathbb{R}$).
