---
title: "Subbasis of a topology"
description: "A collection of sets whose finite intersections form a basis."
---

Let $X$ be a set. A **subbasis** $\mathcal{S}$ for a topology on $X$ is a collection of subsets of $X$ such that the family of all finite {{< knowl id="intersection" text="intersections" >}} of elements of $\mathcal{S}$ forms a {{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis" >}}.

The topology generated by $\mathcal{S}$ is obtained by first taking all finite intersections of subbasic sets (to get a basis) and then taking arbitrary {{< knowl id="union" text="unions" >}} of those basis elements; this is a special case of {{< knowl id="topology-generated-by-basis" text="topology generation by a basis" >}}.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$, the rays $(-\infty,a)$ and $(a,\infty)$ form a subbasis for the usual topology.
- For products, a natural subbasis is given by inverse images of open sets under the coordinate projections (used in the {{< knowl id="product-topology" text="product topology" >}}).
