---
title: "Product topology"
description: "The topology on a product space generated by open rectangles."
---

Let $X$ and $Y$ be {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological spaces" >}}. The **product topology** on the {{< knowl id="cartesian-product" text="Cartesian product" >}} $X\times Y$ is the topology generated by the basis
$$
\mathcal{B}=\{U\times V : U\subseteq X \text{ open in } X,\; V\subseteq Y \text{ open in } Y\}.
$$

In this topology, the coordinate projections $X\times Y\to X$ and $X\times Y\to Y$ are {{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuous" >}}, and a typical basic open set is an “open rectangle” built from {{< knowl id="open-set" text="open sets" >}} in each factor.

**Examples:**
- The usual topology on $\mathbb{R}^2$ is the product topology from the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$.
- If $X$ and $Y$ are discrete, then $X\times Y$ is discrete in the product topology.
