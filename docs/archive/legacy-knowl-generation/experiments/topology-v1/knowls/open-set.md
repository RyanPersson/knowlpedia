---
title: "Open set"
description: "A subset designated as open by a topology."
---

Given a {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}} $(X,\tau)$, a subset $U\subseteq X$ is **open** if $U\in \tau$.

Open sets encode the “allowable” local regions around points; they are used to define {{< knowl id="neighborhood" text="neighborhoods" >}} and {{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuity" >}} purely in terms of set operations.

**Examples:**
- In the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$, each open interval $(a,b)$ is open.
- In the discrete topology on $X$, every subset of $X$ is open.
- In any topology, $\varnothing$ and $X$ are always open.
