---
title: "Closed set"
description: "A subset whose complement is open."
---

In a topological space $(X,\tau)$, a subset $F\subseteq X$ is **closed** if its {{< knowl id="complement" text="complement" >}} $X\setminus F$ is {{< knowl id="open-set" text="open" >}}.

Closed sets are stable under arbitrary intersections and finite unions, and they are the natural language for describing {{< knowl id="closure" text="closures" >}}.

**Examples:**
- In the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$, the interval $[a,b]$ is closed.
- In the discrete topology, every subset is closed.
- In any topology, $\varnothing$ and $X$ are always closed.
