---
title: "Topology generated by a basis"
description: "The smallest topology containing a given basis."
---

Let $\mathcal{B}$ be a {{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis" >}} on a set $X$. The **topology generated by $\mathcal{B}$**, denoted $\tau_{\mathcal{B}}$, is the collection of all sets that can be written as a {{< knowl id="union" text="union" >}} of elements of $\mathcal{B}$.

This is the smallest {{< knowl id="topology" text="topology" >}} on $X$ for which every $B\in\mathcal{B}$ is open, and it is the standard way to define a topology from “basic open sets.”

**Examples:**
- The open intervals generate the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$.
- In a metric space, unions of open balls give exactly the metric-induced topology.
