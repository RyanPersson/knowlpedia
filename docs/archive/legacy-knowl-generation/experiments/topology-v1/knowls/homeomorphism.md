---
title: "Homeomorphism"
description: "A bijective continuous map with continuous inverse."
---

A **homeomorphism** between topological spaces $X$ and $Y$ is a {{< knowl id="bijective-function" text="bijection" >}} $f:X\to Y$ that is {{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuous" >}} and whose {{< knowl id="inverse-function" text="inverse function" >}} $f^{-1}:Y\to X$ is also continuous.

If such a map exists, $X$ and $Y$ are *homeomorphic*, meaning they have the same topological structure (they are “the same” up to relabeling that preserves open sets).

**Examples:**
- The map $(0,1)\to \mathbb{R}$ given by $x\mapsto \tan(\pi(x-\tfrac12))$ is a homeomorphism in the usual topologies.
- Any bijection between two discrete spaces is a homeomorphism.
