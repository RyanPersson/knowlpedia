---
title: "Continuous image of compact is closed and bounded"
description: "A continuous image of a compact set in a metric space is closed and bounded"
---

**Continuous image of compact is closed and bounded**: Let $f:X\to Y$ be continuous, let $K\subseteq X$ be compact, and suppose $Y$ is a {{< knowl id="metric-space" text="metric space" >}}. Then $f(K)$ is closed and bounded in $Y$.

This is a direct consequence of {{< knowl id="continuous-image-of-compact-set-is-compact" text="continuous images of compact sets are compact" >}} together with the metric facts {{< knowl id="compactness-implies-closedness" text="compactness implies closedness" >}} and {{< knowl id="compactness-implies-boundedness" text="compactness implies boundedness" >}}.

**Examples:**
- If $f:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous, then $f([0,1])$ is a closed and bounded subset of $\mathbb{R}$.
- If $f:K\to\mathbb{R}^n$ is continuous and $K$ is compact, then $f(K)$ is bounded and contains all its limit points.
