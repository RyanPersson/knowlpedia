---
title: "Connected subsets of R are intervals"
description: "A connected subset of the real line must be an interval"
---

**Connected subsets of $\mathbb{R}$ are intervals:** Let $A\subseteq \mathbb{R}$ be connected (with the {{< knowl id="subspace-topology" section="topology-core" text="subspace topology" >}} from the usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$). Then $A$ is an interval in the following sense: whenever $a,b\in A$ with $a<b$, every real number $t$ satisfying $a<t<b$ also lies in $A$.

This characterization is the key topological input behind the {{< knowl id="intermediate-value-property-topological" text="intermediate value property" >}} and is often used via the equivalent tests collected in {{< knowl id="connectedness-criteria-r" text="connectedness criteria in R" >}}.
