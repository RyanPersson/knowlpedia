---
title: "Product topology"
description: "The standard topology on a Cartesian product generated by coordinatewise open sets."
---

The **product topology** on a {{< knowl id="cartesian-product" section="shared-foundations" text="Cartesian product" >}} $\prod_{i\in I} X_i$ of {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological spaces" >}} $\{X_i\}_{i\in I}$ is the topology generated by the subbasis
\[
\{\;\pi_i^{-1}(U) : i\in I,\ U \text{ open in } X_i\;\},
\]
where $\pi_i:\prod_{j\in I}X_j\to X_i$ is the $i$th coordinate projection.

With this topology, each projection $\pi_i$ is {{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuous" >}}, and a typical basic open set is specified by choosing open sets in finitely many coordinates and leaving the rest equal to the whole space.

**Examples:**
- For two spaces $X$ and $Y$, a basis for $X\times Y$ is given by sets $U\times V$ with $U$ open in $X$ and $V$ open in $Y$.
- $\mathbb{R}^n$ with its usual topology is the product of $n$ copies of $\mathbb{R}$.
- If $X$ and $Y$ are discrete and the product is finite, then $X\times Y$ is discrete in the product topology.
