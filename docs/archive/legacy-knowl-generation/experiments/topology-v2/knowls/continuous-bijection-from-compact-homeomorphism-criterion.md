---
title: "Continuous bijection from compact space is a homeomorphism (Hausdorff target)"
description: "A continuous bijection from a compact space to a Hausdorff space is a homeomorphism"
---

**Homeomorphism criterion (compact to Hausdorff):** Let $X$ be {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}} and let $Y$ be {{< knowl id="hausdorff-space" section="topology-separation" text="Hausdorff" >}}. If $f:X\to Y$ is a {{< knowl id="continuous-map" section="topology-core" text="continuous" >}} {{< knowl id="bijective-function" section="shared-foundations" text="bijection" >}}, then $f$ is a {{< knowl id="homeomorphism" section="topology-core" text="homeomorphism" >}}.

A common rephrasing is that such an $f$ is automatically a closed map because it carries compact sets to compact sets, and compact sets are closed in Hausdorff spaces.
