---
title: "Compactness criteria in R^k"
description: "Equivalent characterizations of compact subsets of Euclidean space"
---

**Compactness criteria in $\mathbb R^k$:** For a set $K\subseteq \mathbb R^k$ with its usual topology, the following are equivalent:
1. $K$ is {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}} (every {{< knowl id="open-cover" text="open cover" >}} has a finite subcover).
2. $K$ is {{< knowl id="closed-set" section="topology-core" text="closed" >}} and {{< knowl id="bounded-set" section="topology-metric" text="bounded" >}}.
3. $K$ is {{< knowl id="sequentially-compact-set" text="sequentially compact" >}} (every sequence in $K$ has a convergent subsequence with limit in $K$).
4. Every family of closed subsets of $K$ with the {{< knowl id="finite-intersection-property-theorem" text="finite intersection property" >}} has nonempty intersection.

The equivalence of (1) and (2) is the {{< knowl id="heine-borel-theorem" text="Heine–Borel theorem" >}}, and (1) and (3) agree because $\mathbb R^k$ is a metric space.
