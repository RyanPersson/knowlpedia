---
title: "Open set"
description: "A subset that belongs to the topology of a topological space."
---

An **open set** in a {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}} $(X,\tau)$ is a subset $U\subseteq X$ such that $U\in\tau$.

Open sets encode the notion of “nearness” and are used to define {{< knowl id="neighborhood" text="neighborhoods" >}} and {{< knowl id="continuous-map" text="continuity" >}}. Complements of open sets are {{< knowl id="closed-set" text="closed" >}}.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual topology, $(0,1)$ is open, and any union of open intervals is open.
- $\varnothing$ and $X$ are open in every topological space.
- In a {{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" section="topology-metric" text="metric-induced topology" >}}, every open ball $B(x,r)$ is open.
