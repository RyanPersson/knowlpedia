---
title: "Topology generated by a basis"
description: "The smallest topology in which every basis element is open."
---

Given a basis $\mathcal{B}$ on a set $X$ (as in {{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis of a topology" >}}), the **topology generated by $\mathcal{B}$** is
\[
\tau_{\mathcal{B}}=\left\{\,\bigcup_{i\in I} B_i : B_i\in\mathcal{B}\,\right\}.
\]

This is the smallest {{< knowl id="topology" text="topology" >}} on $X$ containing $\mathcal{B}$, and its {{< knowl id="open-set" text="open sets" >}} are precisely the unions of basis elements.

**Examples:**
- The usual topology on $\mathbb{R}$ is generated by the basis of open intervals $(a,b)$.
- The {{< knowl id="metric-induced-topology" section="topology-metric" text="metric-induced topology" >}} is generated by open balls.
- If $\mathcal{B}$ is the set of all singletons in $X$, then $\tau_{\mathcal{B}}$ is the discrete topology.
