---
title: "Dense subset"
description: "A subset that is dense in a given topological space."
---

A **dense subset** of a {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}} $X$ is a subset $A\subseteq X$ such that $\overline{A}=X$, where $\overline{A}$ is the {{< knowl id="closure" text="closure" >}} of $A$.

Dense subsets are often used to compare spaces and to describe how a space can be approximated by a smaller set inside it.

**Examples:**
- $\mathbb{Q}$ is a dense subset of $\mathbb{R}$ (usual topology).
- $(0,1)$ is a dense subset of $[0,1]$ when $[0,1]$ has the {{< knowl id="subspace-topology" text="subspace topology" >}} from $\mathbb{R}$.
- A closed proper subset of a $T_1$ space cannot be dense.
