---
title: "Subbasis of a topology"
description: "A collection of sets whose finite intersections generate a topology."
---

A **subbasis** on a set $X$ is a collection $\mathcal{S}$ of subsets of $X\!$ such that the family of all finite intersections of elements of $\mathcal{S}$ forms a {{< knowl id="basis-of-a-topology" text="basis" >}}.

Equivalently, the topology generated by $\mathcal{S}$ consists of all unions of finite intersections of sets from $\mathcal{S}$ (including $X$, viewed as the empty intersection).

**Examples:**
- For a product $X\times Y$, sets of the form $U\times Y$ and $X\times V$ (with $U$ open in $X$ and $V$ open in $Y$) form a subbasis for the {{< knowl id="product-topology" text="product topology" >}}.
- On any set $X$, the entire topology itself is a subbasis (trivially).
- If $\mathcal{S}$ is too small (e.g., $\mathcal{S}=\{\varnothing\}$), it generates only the indiscrete topology.
