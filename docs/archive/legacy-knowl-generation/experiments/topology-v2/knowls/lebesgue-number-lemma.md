---
title: "Lebesgue number lemma"
description: "A compact metric space admits a uniform ball size subordinate to any open cover"
---

**Lebesgue number lemma:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}, let $K\subseteq X$ be {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}, and let $\mathcal U$ be an {{< knowl id="open-cover" text="open cover" >}} of $K$. Then there exists $\delta>0$ such that for every $x\in K$ there is some $U\in\mathcal U$ with
\[
B(x,\delta)\subseteq U,
\]
where $B(x,\delta)$ is the {{< knowl id="open-ball" section="topology-metric" text="open ball" >}}.

Equivalently, every subset of $K$ of diameter less than $\delta$ is contained in some member of the cover.
