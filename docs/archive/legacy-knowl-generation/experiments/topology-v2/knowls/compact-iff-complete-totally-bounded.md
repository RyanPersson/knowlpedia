---
title: "Compact iff complete and totally bounded"
description: "In metric spaces, compactness is equivalent to completeness plus total boundedness"
---

**Compact iff complete and totally bounded:** Let $(X,d)$ be a {{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}} and let $K\subseteq X$. Then $K$ is {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}} if and only if $K$ is complete (with the subspace metric) and {{< knowl id="totally-bounded-set" text="totally bounded" >}}.

One direction combines {{< knowl id="compactness-implies-completeness" text="compactness implies completeness" >}} and {{< knowl id="compactness-implies-total-boundedness" text="compactness implies total boundedness" >}}; the other direction uses {{< knowl id="totally-bounded-cauchy-subsequence" text="Cauchy subsequences in totally bounded sets" >}} plus completeness.
