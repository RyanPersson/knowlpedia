---
title: "Closed set"
description: "A subset whose complement is open."
---

A **closed set** in a {{< knowl id="topological-space" text="topological space" >}} $(X,\tau)$ is a subset $F\subseteq X$ such that $X\setminus F$ is an {{< knowl id="open-set" text="open set" >}}.

Equivalently, $F$ is closed if it is the {{< knowl id="complement" section="shared-foundations" text="complement" >}} (in $X$) of some open set. Closed sets are central to the definitions of {{< knowl id="closure" text="closure" >}} and {{< knowl id="limit-point-accumulation-point-cluster-point" text="limit points" >}}.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb{R}$ with the usual topology, $[0,1]$ is closed.
- $\varnothing$ and $X$ are closed in every topological space.
- In the indiscrete topology $\{\varnothing,X\}$, the only closed sets are $\varnothing$ and $X$.
