---
title: "Metric-induced topology"
description: "The topology on a metric space defined by its open balls"
---

A **metric-induced topology** is defined as follows. Let $(X,d)$ be a {{< knowl id="metric-space" text="metric space" >}}. A subset $U\subseteq X$ is declared open if for every $x\in U$ there exists $r>0$ such that $B_d(x,r)\subseteq U$, where $B_d(x,r)$ is the {{< knowl id="open-ball" text="open ball" >}} of radius $r$ around $x$.

The collection $\mathcal{T}_d$ of all such sets $U$ is a {{< knowl id="topology" section="topology-core" text="topology" >}} on $X$, and its elements are precisely the {{< knowl id="open-set" section="topology-core" text="open sets" >}} determined by the metric.

**Examples:**
- On $\mathbb{R}$ with $d(x,y)=|x-y|$, $\mathcal{T}_d$ is the usual topology generated by open intervals.
- For the discrete metric on $X$, every subset of $X$ is open (so $\mathcal{T}_d$ is the discrete topology).
- If $d$ and $d'$ are {{< knowl id="equivalent-metrics" text="equivalent metrics" >}} on $X$, then they induce the same topology: $\mathcal{T}_d=\mathcal{T}_{d'}$.
