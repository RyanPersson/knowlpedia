---
title: "Open cover"
description: "A family of open sets whose union contains a given set"
---

An **open cover** of a set $A$ in a {{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}} $X$ is a family of {{< knowl id="open-set" section="topology-core" text="open sets" >}} $\mathcal U=\{U_i\}_{i\in I}$ such that
\[
A \subseteq \bigcup_{i\in I} U_i.
\]
Equivalently, $\mathcal U$ is a {{< knowl id="cover" section="topology-core" text="cover" >}} of $A$ by open sets.

**Examples:**
- In $\mathbb R$ with its usual topology, the family $\{(-n,n)\}_{n\in\mathbb N}$ is an open cover of $\mathbb R$.
- For $A=[0,1]\subset \mathbb R$, the family $\{(-1/2,2),\,(-1,1/2),\,(1/2,3/2)\}$ is an open cover of $A$.
