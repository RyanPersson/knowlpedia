---
title: "Uniqueness of limits in Hausdorff spaces"
description: "In a Hausdorff space, a convergent sequence has at most one limit"
---

**Uniqueness of limits in Hausdorff spaces:** Let $X$ be a {{< knowl id="hausdorff-space" text="Hausdorff space" >}} and let $(x_n)$ be a sequence in $X$. If $x_n\to x$ and $x_n\to y$, then $x=y$.

This is one of the main practical consequences of Hausdorffness: it makes the notation “$\lim x_n$” unambiguous whenever a limit exists (for example in any {{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}}).

**Key insight:** If $x\neq y$, Hausdorffness provides disjoint neighborhoods of $x$ and $y$, and a sequence cannot be eventually contained in both disjoint sets.
