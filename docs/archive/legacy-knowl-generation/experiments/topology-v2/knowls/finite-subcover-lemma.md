---
title: "Finite subcover lemma"
description: "A compact set has a finite subcover from any open cover"
---

**Finite subcover lemma:** Let $K$ be a {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact set" >}} in a {{< knowl id="topological-space" section="topology-core" text="topological space" >}} $X$. For every {{< knowl id="open-cover" text="open cover" >}} $\mathcal U=\{U_i\}_{i\in I}$ of $K$, there exist indices $i_1,\dots,i_n\in I$ such that
\[
K \subseteq U_{i_1}\cup\cdots\cup U_{i_n}.
\]

This is the defining covering property of compactness, and it is often used together with {{< knowl id="refinement-of-an-open-cover" text="refinements" >}} of covers.
