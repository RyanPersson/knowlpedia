---
title: "Compactness implies total boundedness"
description: "In metric spaces, compact sets are totally bounded"
---

**Compactness implies total boundedness:** If $(X,d)$ is a {{< knowl id="metric-space" section="topology-metric" text="metric space" >}} and $K\subseteq X$ is {{< knowl id="compact-set" text="compact" >}}, then $K$ is {{< knowl id="totally-bounded-set" text="totally bounded" >}}.

This is a key step toward the metric characterization {{< knowl id="compact-iff-complete-totally-bounded" text="compact iff complete and totally bounded" >}}.
